Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is ELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA player who, at the time of signing, has exactly five (5) Years of Service
BA player who, at the time of signing, has exactly four (4) Years of Service
CA player who, at the time of signing, has exactly three (3) Years of Service
DA player who, at the time of signing, has exactly six (6) Years of Service
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) expressly prohibits a trade bonus in any Contract or Extension signed by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service. Only a player with six (6) or more Years of Service at signing is eligible.
Q2Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rule for the THIRD and FOURTH Seasons of such Contract?
AThe Base Salary for the third and fourth Seasons is automatically fully protected upon signing
BThe Base Salary for the third and fourth Seasons becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the third Regular Season covered by such Contract
CThe Base Salary for the third and fourth Seasons becomes fully protected if the option for the fourth Season is exercised by the Team
DSuch a Rookie Scale Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the third or fourth Seasons
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii) provides that for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, the Base Salary for each of the third and fourth Seasons shall become fully protected if the option for the fourth Season of such Contract is exercised by the Team.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), which specific category of suspended player is expressly carved out of the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection of any kind?
AA player suspended by the WNBA for conduct detrimental to the league for any duration
BA player who has been suspended for the entire final Season of her Player Contract pursuant to Art. XIV, Sec. 6 or Art. XX, Sec. 1(c)
CA player suspended without pay under Art. XIV, Sec. 9 for failing to report at the start of a Season
DA player suspended pending investigation under the Joint Domestic Violence Policy
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(ii) expressly provides that the Player Contract of a player who has been suspended for the entire final Season of such Contract pursuant to Art. XIV, Sec. 6 or Art. XX, Sec. 1(c) shall not count against the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection of any kind.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays four (4) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated, then one (1) Season under an Extension of that Contract. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays two (2) Seasons under her Contract (no extension). How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
ASeven (7) Seasons
BSix (6) Seasons
CFive (5) Seasons
DFour (4) Seasons
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that only Seasons played under the Contract signed while designated—but NOT Seasons covered by any Extension—count toward the maximum. Player A contributes four (4) Seasons (extension Season excluded) and Player B contributes two (2) Seasons, for a total of six (6).
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) and Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Veteran Free Agent with exactly three (3) Years of Service is eligible for what type of qualifying offer, and what is the salary floor for that offer?
AShe is eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer; the floor is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of her prior Contract
BShe is not eligible for any Qualifying Offer under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) because she has fewer than four (4) Years of Service; she becomes an Unrestricted Free Agent
CShe is eligible for a Core Qualifying Offer equal to the Maximum Player Salary
DShe is eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer set at 105% of her prior Season Base Salary
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) expressly requires that a Veteran Free Agent have four (4) or more Years of Service to be eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer under that Section. A player with only three (3) Years of Service does not qualify and becomes an Unrestricted Free Agent.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(c)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), what is the Minimum Team Salary as a percentage of the Salary Cap, and which of the following correctly states the ONE recognized exception to the WNBA's obligation to cause a Team to make Minimum Team Salary shortfall payments?
A80% of the Salary Cap; a Team that has fewer than ten (10) players on its roster at the conclusion of the Season is exempt
B85% of the Salary Cap; a Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season is not required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation
C90% of the Salary Cap; a Team that has been granted a Hardship Exception during the Season is exempt
D85% of the Salary Cap; an Expansion Team in its first Season of operations is exempt
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap. Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(ii) provides the sole express exception: any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season shall not be required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation.

Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states the out-of-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the percentage of eligible charges covered BEFORE the out-of-pocket threshold is reached?
A$2,500 deductible per covered individual; 70% coverage for most eligible charges and 80% for certain other charges
B$1,800 deductible per covered individual; 60% coverage for most eligible charges
C$2,500 deductible per covered individual; 60% coverage for most eligible charges and 70% for certain other eligible charges

CBA Reference
Art. Q8Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2030 Salary Cap Year?
A30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
B30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
C30.25% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
D30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) expressly provides that for the 2030 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year.
Q9Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), the Rookie Scale Amounts for Second Round Picks and Third Round Picks in Rookie Scale Contracts commencing with Seasons after 2026 are adjusted by the same percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap that applies to First Round Picks.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) differentiates the two mechanisms: for First Round Picks the adjustment equals the percentage increase or decrease in the Salary Cap, while for Second Round Picks and Third Round Picks the Rookie Scale Amounts are instead increased by a fixed four percent (4%), regardless of Salary Cap movement.
Q10Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, if a Team fines a player the maximum 2.5% per-game amount for missing Regular Season games, the 20% aggregate fine cap expressly does NOT limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed for the same missed games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 explicitly states that 'nothing in this Section 7 shall be construed to limit a Team's right...to impose a suspension or other discipline on a player for the same (or other) missed games in accordance with this Agreement (and the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension).' Therefore the statement is TRUE.
