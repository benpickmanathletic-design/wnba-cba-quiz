Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), a player's Contract is suspended for the entire final Season pursuant to Article XX, Section 1(c). Which of the following correctly states how that Contract is treated with respect to the seven (7) Player Contract limit for Base Salary protection?
AA) It counts against the limit because the suspension does not arise under Article XIV, Section 6.
BB) It does not count against the limit, because Art. V, Sec. 3(b) expressly excludes Contracts suspended for the entire final Season pursuant to Article XIV, Section 6 OR Article XX, Section 1(c).
CC) It counts against the limit until the suspension is lifted, after which it no longer counts.
DD) It does not count against the limit only if the Base Salary protection is for lack of skill, not injury.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b) expressly provides that a Player Contract of a player suspended for the entire final Season pursuant to Article XIV, Section 6 OR Article XX, Section 1(c) shall not count against the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection of any kind.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), which of the following is CORRECT regarding a Contract that already contains a trade bonus when it is traded, and the player and the assignor Team wish to modify that bonus in connection with the trade?
AA) A Contract containing a trade bonus may never be amended with respect to that bonus under any circumstances.
BB) In connection with the trade of the Contract, the player and the assignor Team may agree to amend the Contract to waive all or any portion of the trade bonus.
CC) The trade bonus may be increased in connection with a trade, provided the player has at least six (6) Years of Service.
DD) The assignee Team, not the assignor Team, must consent to any modification of the trade bonus.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(v) provides that a Contract containing a trade bonus cannot be amended to change such trade bonus, EXCEPT that in connection with the trade of the Contract, the player and the assignor Team may agree to amend the Contract to waive all or any portion of the trade bonus. No other modifications are permitted.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Lottery First Round Pick's Rookie Scale Contract, which of the following correctly describes when the Base Salary for the SECOND Season becomes fully protected?
AA) The second Season Base Salary is fully protected upon signing, just like the first Season.
BB) The second Season Base Salary becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CC) The second Season Base Salary becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the option for the fourth Season.
DD) The second Season Base Salary becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(i) states that for a Lottery First Round Pick, the Base Salary for the second Season shall become fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by the Contract. This differs from non-Lottery First Round Picks, whose first Season protection is also conditional.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), which of the following MOST accurately describes the treatment of Seasons played under an Extension of a Core Player Contract toward the maximum number of Core Player designations a Team may make?
AA) All Seasons played under both the original Core Player Contract and any Extension count toward the maximum.
BB) Only Seasons played while the player was designated as a Core Player under the original Contract count toward the maximum; Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract are excluded.
CC) Seasons played under an Extension count toward the maximum only if the Extension was negotiated during the Moratorium Period.
DD) No Seasons played under any Core Player Contract or Extension count toward the maximum; the designation itself is what counts.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that Seasons played while a player was designated as a Core Player (but NOT including Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract) shall be included in the maximum number of Core Player designations. This means Extension Seasons are expressly carved out.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service is eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer. Under Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' which of the following correctly states the salary floor for such an offer?
AA) 105% of the player's fourth year Base Salary under her expiring Rookie Scale Contract.
BB) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
CC) The Standard Maximum Salary for the applicable Salary Cap Year.
DD) The Minimum Annual Salary for a player with 1–3 Years of Service.
CBA Reference
Per Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Restricted Qualifying Offer for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d) is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract. This baseline ensures the offer is at least as high as the player's most recent salary.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Guarantee Shortfall' arises when which of the following conditions is met at the conclusion of a Season?
AA) A Team's Team Salary falls below 85% of the Salary Cap for the applicable Salary Cap Year.
BB) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year is less than the Salary Cap for that year multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in that Season.
CC) The Salary Cap for a Salary Cap Year would have increased by more than 13% from the prior year but for the cap on increases.
DD) Any single Team's Team Salary exceeds the Salary Cap for the applicable Salary Cap Year without a valid Exception.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) defines the 'Guarantee Shortfall' as arising when, as of the conclusion of a Season, Total Salaries for such Salary Cap Year is less than the Salary Cap for such Salary Cap Year multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in such Season. This is distinct from the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall and the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
Q7Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2029 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) expressly states that for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year. This differs from 2028 (which uses 12% Team Revenue) and 2030 (which uses 12.5% Team Revenue).
Q8Art. X Sec. 1(a)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), which of the following correctly describes the out-of-network coverage structure for non-Toronto Tempo players under the WNBA medical plan?
AA) $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual; 70% coverage for all eligible charges until $8,000 out-of-pocket; 100% thereafter.
BB) $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual; 60% for most eligible charges and 70% for certain other eligible charges until $8,000 out-of-pocket per person; 100% thereafter.
CC) $1,800 annual deductible per covered individual; 60% coverage until $8,000 out-of-pocket; 100% thereafter.
DD) $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual; 80% coverage until $8,000 out-of-pocket per person; 100% thereafter.
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) provides that for non-Toronto Tempo players, out-of-network services carry a $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual ($6,250 per family), coverage at 60% for most eligible charges and 70% for certain other eligible charges until the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person ($20,000 per family), and 100% thereafter with no annual or lifetime maximum benefit.
Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2026, 2027, and 2028 Seasons, ALL players who are not Developmental Players are entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of their WNBA Team, regardless of their Base Salary level.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(ii) expressly states that for each of the 2026, 2027, and 2028 Seasons, ALL players who are not Developmental Players will be provided with a one-bedroom apartment in the home city of their WNBA Team, with no Base Salary threshold. The $500,000 salary threshold only applies beginning in the 2029 and 2030 Seasons under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii).
Q10Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), Rookie Scale Amounts for Second Round Picks in Seasons after the 2026 Season are adjusted by the same percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap that applies to First Round Picks, rather than by a fixed 4% annual increase.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) expressly distinguishes the two adjustment methods: First Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by the percentage increase or decrease in the Salary Cap, while Second Round Pick (and other) Rookie Scale Amounts are increased by a fixed 4% annually. This is a key structural difference introduced in the 2026 CBA.
