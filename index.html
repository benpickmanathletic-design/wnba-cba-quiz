Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(ii) and (vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii), a trade bonus in a Contract may not exceed what percentage of the Base Salary remaining to be earned by the player at the time of the trade, and in no event may that bonus be payable more than how many times?
AA. 15% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than twice
BB. 10% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than once
CC. 10% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than twice
DD. 5% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than once
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii) caps a trade bonus at ten percent (10%) of the Base Salary remaining to be earned at the time of the trade, and Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) confirms the bonus may not be payable more than once.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is INELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA. A player who, at signing, has exactly six (6) Years of Service
BB. A player who, at signing, has exactly seven (7) Years of Service
CC. A player who, at signing, has exactly nine (9) Years of Service
DD. A player who, at signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) expressly prohibits any Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service from containing a trade bonus. Players with six or more Years of Service are eligible.

AA. All Seasons played under both the original Contract and any Extension count toward the limit.
BB. Only Seasons played while the player was designated as a Core Player under the original Contract (not including Seasons covered by any Extension) count toward the limit.
CC. Only the Seasons played during the extended term count toward the limit, not the original Contract Seasons.
DD. No Seasons under any Contract or Extension count toward the limit once the player signs a new Contract.

Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) specifies that only the Seasons played while the player was designated as a Core Player — but NOT including Seasons covered by any Extension to that Contract — count toward the maximum number of players who may be designated as Core Players by that Team.

Q8Art. VIII Sec. (c)4-choice
Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), for Rookie Scale Contracts commencing with Seasons after 2026, Second Round Pick and Third Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by which mechanism, and by what amount?
AA. Adjusted by the percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap, the same as First Round Picks.
BB. Adjusted by a fixed 4% increase each Season, regardless of changes to the Salary Cap.
CC. Adjusted by the percentage increase (or decrease) in the Consumer Price Index.
DD. Not adjusted at all; they remain fixed at $270,000 for each Season of the Agreement.
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) states that for Second Round Picks (and others not classified as First Round Picks), the Rookie Scale Amounts for Contracts commencing after 2026 are adjusted by a flat 4% increase — unlike First Round Picks, whose amounts track the percentage change in the Salary Cap.
Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a non-Developmental Player whose Base Salary is exactly $500,001 is entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of her WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) limits the Team-provided one-bedroom apartment entitlement for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons to non-Developmental Players whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000. A player earning $500,001 exceeds that threshold and is therefore not entitled to Team-provided housing under this provision.
Q10Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, the per-playoff-game fine cap is 5% of Base Salary and the aggregate fine cap for missing playoff games in any Season is 25% of Base Salary; furthermore, these Section 7 fine caps do NOT limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed for the same missed playoff games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 confirms that the per-playoff-game fine cap is 5% of Base Salary and the aggregate cap is 25% of Base Salary for missed playoff games. The section expressly clarifies that the 25% cap does not apply to lost salary from a suspension, meaning suspension reductions are separate from and in addition to Section 7 fines.
