Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is ELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player who, at the time of signing, has five (5) Years of Service
BB) A player who, at the time of signing, has four (4) Years of Service
CC) A player who, at the time of signing, has six (6) Years of Service
DD) A Rookie signing her initial Rookie Scale Contract regardless of Years of Service
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) expressly prohibits a trade bonus in any Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service. Therefore, only a player with at least six (6) Years of Service at signing is eligible.
Q2Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rule for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected immediately upon signing
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by the Contract
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind
DD) The Base Salary for the first Season is protected only against injury, not lack of skill
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii) explicitly states that a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick 'may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season of such Contract.' Protection begins only in the second Season if waivers are not requested before the first day of that second Regular Season.
Q3Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays three (3) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated and two (2) Seasons under an Extension of that Contract. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays one (1) Season under her Contract (no extension). How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) Six (6) Seasons total
BB) Four (4) Seasons total
CC) Five (5) Seasons total
DD) Three (3) Seasons total
CBA Reference
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), only Seasons played under the Contract signed while the player was designated as a Core Player count — Seasons under Extensions do NOT count. Player A contributes three (3) Seasons and Player B contributes one (1) Season, for a total of four (4) Seasons.
Q4Art. I (Qualifying Offer); Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) and Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Veteran Free Agent with exactly nine (9) Years of Service receives a Restricted Qualifying Offer from her Prior Team. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) The Standard Maximum Salary applicable to the Salary Cap Year in which the offer is made
BB) The Supermax Salary applicable to the Salary Cap Year in which the offer is made
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
DD) 105% of the Base Salary in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' sub-clause (D) states that for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), a Restricted Qualifying Offer must be the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract. Years of Service beyond four (4) do not alter this formula.
Q5Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii), what specific condition triggers a 'Guarantee Shortfall' for a Salary Cap Year, and how is it computed?
AA) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year exceed the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams; the shortfall equals the excess amount
BB) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in that Season; the shortfall equals that difference
CC) Total Salaries fall below 85% of the Salary Cap multiplied by total Teams; the shortfall equals the gap between actual salaries and the 85% floor
DD) The Salary Cap would have increased by more than 10% but for the cap on increases; the shortfall equals the capped amount multiplied by total Teams
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) provides that a 'Guarantee Shortfall' arises when, as of the conclusion of a Season, Total Salaries for that Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap for that year multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in that Season, with the shortfall equaling the amount of that difference.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(c)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), the Minimum Team Salary for a Salary Cap Year equals what percentage of the Salary Cap, and which of the following is the ONE recognized exception to the WNBA's obligation to cause a Team to make up any Minimum Team Salary shortfall?
AA) 80% of the Salary Cap; the exception is a Team that has been sanctioned by the Commissioner for repeated cap violations
BB) 85% of the Salary Cap; the exception is any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season
CC) 85% of the Salary Cap; the exception is any Team with fewer than ten (10) players on its roster at the conclusion of the Season
DD) 90% of the Salary Cap; the exception is any Team that participates in an expansion draft during the Season
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap. Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(ii) expressly states the one exception: any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season shall not be required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
Q7Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2028 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) expressly states that for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year. The 12.25% Team Revenue rate applies to the 2029 Salary Cap Year, not 2028.
Q8Art. XI Sec. 1(a)4-choice
Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2031 and 2032 Seasons, which of the following CORRECTLY describes which non-Developmental Players are entitled to Team-provided housing in the home city?
AA) All non-Developmental Players regardless of Base Salary level
BB) Only non-Developmental Players whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000
CC) Only non-Developmental Players whose Base Salary exceeds $500,000
DD) No non-Developmental Players; housing is eliminated for Seasons after 2030
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a) provides Team-provided housing for all non-Developmental Players for 2026-2028 Seasons, and only for non-Developmental Players with Base Salary at or below $500,000 for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons. There is no provision extending Team-provided housing to any non-Developmental Players for the 2031 or 2032 Seasons.
Q9Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), for Rookie Scale Contracts commencing with Seasons after 2026, the Rookie Scale Amounts applicable to Second Round Picks and Third Round Picks are adjusted by the same percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap that governs First Round Pick adjustments.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
False. Art. VIII, Sec. (c) explicitly provides a two-track adjustment system: First Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by the percentage increase or decrease in the Salary Cap, while Second and Third Round Pick (and 'All Others') Rookie Scale Amounts are instead increased by a fixed 4% regardless of Salary Cap movement.
Q10Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, if a Team fines a player the maximum 2.5%-per-game amount for missing Regular Season games, the 20% aggregate Regular Season fine cap expressly DOES apply to limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed on the player for those same missed Regular Season games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
False. Art. XIV, Sec. 7 expressly states that 'the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension,' making clear that while a Team retains the right to impose a suspension for the same missed games, the aggregate fine cap does not limit the salary reduction resulting from that suspension.
