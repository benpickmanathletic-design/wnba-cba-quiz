Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), a Team currently has six (6) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection. A player signs a new Contract containing Base Salary protection, and that Contract is later extended. During the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract's initial and option term, which of the following is CORRECT regarding that Rookie Scale Contract's effect on the seven (7) Contract limit — and what happens once the extended term begins?
AA) The Rookie Scale Contract counts against the limit during both the initial/option term and the extended term.
BB) The Rookie Scale Contract does not count against the limit during the four Seasons of the initial/option term, but DOES count against the limit during the extended term.
CC) The Rookie Scale Contract never counts against the seven (7) Contract limit, regardless of whether it is extended.
DD) The Rookie Scale Contract counts against the limit only if the Base Salary was renegotiated pursuant to Art. VII, Sec. 5(c)(ii).
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection does not count against the seven (7) Contract limit during the four Seasons of its initial and option term, but this exception 'shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' Once the extension begins, the Contract counts against the cap.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv), a Contract that does NOT contain a trade bonus is extended. Which of the following CORRECTLY describes the conditions under which a trade bonus may be added to that Contract at the time of extension, and when that bonus first becomes payable?
AA) The trade bonus may be added via Exhibit 8 at extension and is payable the first time the Contract is traded, without restriction.
BB) The trade bonus may be added via Exhibit 8 simultaneously with the extension, and is payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term.
CC) The trade bonus may be added at any time after the extension is signed, provided the player has six (6) Years of Service.
DD) A trade bonus may never be added to a Contract that did not originally contain one, even through an extension.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv) provides that a Contract without a trade bonus may be amended simultaneously with an extension to add a trade bonus, but notwithstanding the general 'first trade' rule, the bonus is payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term; the required language must be included in Exhibit 8.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a non-Lottery First Round Pick, which of the following CORRECTLY describes the Base Salary protection status for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon signing, with no conditions.
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if the fourth-year option is exercised.
DD) A non-Lottery First Round Pick's Rookie Scale Contract may not contain any Base Salary protection for the first Season.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(ii) states that for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by any First Round Pick other than a Lottery pick, the Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract — a conditional protection that differs from the unconditional protection afforded to Lottery picks.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent with exactly three (3) Years of Service wishes to become a Restricted Free Agent. Which of the following CORRECTLY states her eligibility under the 2026 CBA?
AA) She is eligible; three (3) Years of Service is sufficient to receive a Restricted Qualifying Offer under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b).
BB) She is ineligible; Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) requires at least four (4) Years of Service to be eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer.
CC) She is eligible only if she was previously subject to a Fourth Year Option under Art. VI, Sec. 4.
DD) She is eligible only if the Team designates her as a Core Player under Art. VI, Sec. 7.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) explicitly requires that a Veteran Free Agent have four (4) or more Years of Service to be eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer under that section; a player with only three (3) Years of Service does not qualify and would instead become an Unrestricted Free Agent.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player and she plays two (2) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated. That Contract is then extended, and she plays three (3) additional Seasons under the extended term. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays one (1) Season under her Contract. How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) Six (6) Seasons — all five of Player A's Seasons plus one of Player B's.
BB) Three (3) Seasons — Player A's two original Seasons plus Player B's one Season; extended-term Seasons never count.
CC) Four (4) Seasons — Player A's two original Seasons, Player B's one Season, and one extended-term Season.
DD) Five (5) Seasons — all of Player A's five Seasons plus Player B's one Season minus one for the extension.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that only Seasons played under a Contract signed while the player was designated (but NOT Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract) count toward the maximum number of Core Player designations. Player A's two original Seasons and Player B's one Season total three (3); Player A's three extended-term Seasons are excluded.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for the 2027 Salary Cap Year arises when which of the following conditions is met?
AA) The Salary Cap for 2027 would have increased by more than ten percent (10%) from the 2026 Salary Cap but for the operation of the cap growth limit.
BB) The Salary Cap for 2027 would have increased by more than thirteen percent (13%) from the 2026 Salary Cap but for the operation of the cap growth limit.
CC) Total Salaries for 2027 fall below the Salary Cap multiplied by the number of Teams.
DD) Any individual Team's Team Salary falls below eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) specifies that the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall for the 2027 Salary Cap Year is triggered specifically when the cap would have increased by more than thirteen percent (13%) from 2026 but for the operation of Sec. 1(a)(ii), which caps 2027's growth at 13% — distinct from the 10% limit that applies to all other Salary Cap Years.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states the out-of-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the out-of-pocket threshold at which out-of-network coverage reaches 100% under the WNBA medical plan?
AA) $2,500 annual deductible; 100% coverage after the individual has paid $6,000 out-of-pocket.
BB) $1,800 annual deductible; 100% coverage after the individual has paid $8,000 out-of-pocket.
CC) $2,500 annual deductible; 100% coverage after the individual has paid $8,000 out-of-pocket.
DD) $600 annual deductible; 100% coverage after the individual has paid $8,000 out-of-pocket.
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) specifies that for non-Toronto Tempo players, out-of-network services carry a $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual and coverage reaches 100% once the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person in a calendar year; the $600 deductible and $3,000 threshold apply only to in-network services.

Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2029 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) explicitly states that for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year — distinct from the 2028 formula (which uses 12% of Team Revenue) and the 2030 formula (which uses 12.5%).
Q9Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, if a Team (or the WNBA) fines a player for missing both Regular Season games and playoff games in the same Season, the aggregate fine cap for missed Regular Season games (20% of Base Salary) and the aggregate fine cap for missed playoff games (25% of Base Salary) are each applied independently, meaning the total fines could reach up to 45% of the player's Base Salary.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 sets separate, independent caps of 20% of Base Salary for missing Regular Season games and 25% for missing playoff games in any Season, with the further clarification that the 20% and 25% caps do not apply to lost salary from suspensions. The two caps operate independently and do not merge, so in theory both caps could be reached simultaneously for a maximum combined fine of 45% of Base Salary.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a player who is NOT a Developmental Player and whose Base Salary is exactly $500,000 is entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of her WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) states that for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, players (who are not Developmental Players) whose Base Salary is 'less than or equal to $500,000' are entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment; a player earning exactly $500,000 falls within this threshold and therefore qualifies.
