Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv), a Player Contract that does NOT already contain a trade bonus may be amended to add one only under what specific circumstance, and what restriction applies to when that added bonus is payable?
AA) Only if the Contract is assigned to another Team; the bonus is payable only upon the second subsequent trade
BB) Only if the Contract is extended; the bonus shall be payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term
CC) Only if the player has at least six (6) Years of Service at the time of the amendment; the bonus is payable upon the very next trade
DD) Only if both parties consent in writing before the Moratorium Period; the bonus is payable only once during the original Contract term
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv) provides that a Contract without a trade bonus may only be amended to add one in connection with an Extension of that Contract, and the added bonus is payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the first Season of the extended term. This is a specific carve-out to the general rule in subsection (i) that a trade bonus is payable only the first time the Contract is traded.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection does NOT count against a Team's per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts with Base Salary protection. Which of the following MOST PRECISELY describes when this exception CEASES to apply?
AA) When the player's Rookie Scale Contract is terminated by the Team for any reason
BB) When the fourth Season option of the Rookie Scale Contract is exercised by the Team
CC) When the Rookie Scale Contract is extended, the exception no longer applies with respect to or during the extended term
DD) When the player completes two (2) Seasons under the Rookie Scale Contract
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) explicitly states the exception applies 'only with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' Once the Contract is extended, the exception ceases for the extended term.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rule for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested before the first day of the first Regular Season
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon signing the Contract
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season may contain conditional Base Salary protection only
DD) Such a Rookie Scale Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii) expressly states: 'For clarity, such a Rookie Scale Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season of such Contract.' This distinguishes Second and Third Round Picks from First Round Picks, who do receive first-Season protection.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays two (2) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated and then three (3) additional Seasons under an Extension of that Contract. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays one (1) Season under her Contract. How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) Six (6) Seasons
BB) Three (3) Seasons
CC) Five (5) Seasons
DD) Two (2) Seasons
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) states that only Seasons played under the Contract signed while the player was designated — but NOT Seasons covered by any Extension of that Contract — count toward the maximum. For Player A, only 2 Seasons count (the 3 Extension Seasons are excluded). For Player B, 1 Season counts. The total is 3 Seasons.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b); Art. I (Qualifying Offer)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) and Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Veteran Free Agent with exactly six (6) Years of Service receives a Restricted Qualifying Offer from her Prior Team. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) 105% of the player's Base Salary from the last Season of her prior Contract
BB) The applicable Standard Maximum Salary for the Salary Cap Year
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
DD) The applicable Supermax Salary for the Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' subpart (D) provides that for a player subject to a right of first refusal in accordance with Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), the Restricted Qualifying Offer must equal 'the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.' There is no elevation based on Years of Service for this category.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii), the 'Guarantee Shortfall' for a Salary Cap Year is triggered and computed under which of the following conditions?
AA) When the Salary Cap for a Salary Cap Year would have increased by more than 10% but for the cap on increases; the shortfall equals the excess multiplied by the number of Teams
BB) When Total Salaries for a Salary Cap Year is less than the Salary Cap for that Year multiplied by the total number of Teams; the shortfall equals that difference
CC) When a Team's Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary; the shortfall equals the per-team deficiency summed across all Teams
DD) When the WNBA fails to distribute League Shortfall payments within sixty (60) days; the shortfall accrues interest at the prime rate
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) provides that the Guarantee Shortfall arises when, as of the conclusion of a Season, Total Salaries for that Salary Cap Year is less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of participating Teams, and the Guarantee Shortfall equals the amount of that shortfall. This is distinct from the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall under Sec. 1(b)(i).
Q7Art. VII Sec. 1(c)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), which of the following CORRECTLY states BOTH the Minimum Team Salary as a percentage of the Salary Cap AND the ONE recognized exception to the WNBA's obligation to cause a Team to make Minimum Team Salary shortfall payments?
AA) 80% of the Salary Cap; exception for any Team that finishes below .500 in the Regular Season
BB) 85% of the Salary Cap; exception for any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season
CC) 90% of the Salary Cap; exception for any Expansion Team in its first Season of operations
DD) 85% of the Salary Cap; exception for any Team that files for bankruptcy protection during the Season
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at 85% of the Salary Cap. Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(ii) states that the WNBA shall cause shortfall payments to be made, but expressly excepts any Team that 'ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season' from the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
Q8Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2028 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) specifies that for the 2028 Salary Cap Year the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year. Note that the 12.25% Team Revenue rate does not apply until the 2029 Salary Cap Year.
Q9Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, the 20% aggregate Regular Season fine cap and the 25% aggregate playoff fine cap DO apply to limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed for the same missed games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 expressly states that 'the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension.' The caps limit only the Section 7 per-game fines; they do not cap salary reductions arising from a separately imposed suspension for the same missed games.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a non-Developmental Player whose Base Salary is exactly $500,000 is entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of her WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) provides that for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, all non-Developmental Players whose Base Salary is 'less than or equal to $500,000' are entitled to Team-provided housing. Because $500,000 satisfies the 'less than or equal to' threshold, such a player is entitled to the one-bedroom apartment.
