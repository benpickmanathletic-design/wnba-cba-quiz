Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(ii) and 3(g)(v)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii), a trade bonus in a Contract may not exceed what percentage of the Base Salary remaining to be earned by the player at the time of the trade, and in no event may that bonus be payable more than how many times?
AA) 15% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than twice
BB) 10% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than once
CC) 10% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than twice
DD) 15% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than once
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii) states a trade bonus shall not exceed ten percent (10%) of the Base Salary remaining to be earned at the time of the trade, and Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(v) provides that in no event shall a trade bonus in a Contract be payable more than once.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is INELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player entering her seventh Season who has exactly six (6) Years of Service at the time of signing
BB) A player who has exactly seven (7) Years of Service at the time of signing
CC) A player who has exactly five (5) Years of Service at the time of signing
DD) A player who has exactly eight (8) Years of Service at the time of signing
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) explicitly provides that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. A player with exactly five (5) Years of Service therefore falls below the threshold and is ineligible.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rules for the SECOND Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the second Season is fully protected from the moment the Contract is signed
BB) The Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by such Contract
CC) The Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected only if the option for the fourth Season is exercised by the Team
DD) No Base Salary protection of any kind is permitted for the second Season of such a Contract
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, the Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by such Contract. The first Season of such Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays three (3) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated. That Contract is then extended, and she plays two (2) additional Seasons under the extended term. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays one (1) Season under her Contract. How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) Six (6) Seasons
BB) Four (4) Seasons
CC) Five (5) Seasons
DD) Three (3) Seasons
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that only Seasons played under the Contract signed while the player was designated (but NOT Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract) count toward the maximum Core Player designation limit. Therefore, only Player A's three (3) original Contract Seasons plus Player B's one (1) Season count, totaling four (4) Seasons.

AA) 80% of the Salary Cap; exception applies if the Team finishes below .500 in the Regular Season
BB) 85% of the Salary Cap; exception applies if a Team ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season
CC) 90% of the Salary Cap; exception applies if a Team is in its first expansion Season

CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap for such Salary Cap Year. Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(ii) provides that notwithstanding the general shortfall payment obligation, any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season shall not be required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation.

AA) In-network: $1,200 per individual; Out-of-network: $4,000 per individual

Q7Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2031 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) provides that for each of the 2031 and 2032 Salary Cap Years, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the Salary Cap Year immediately preceding the applicable Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the Salary Cap Year immediately preceding the applicable Salary Cap Year. For 2031, that means 30.5% of 2030 League Revenue plus 13% of 2030 Team Revenue.

Q9Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), the Rookie Scale Amounts for Second Round Picks in Rookie Scale Contracts commencing after 2026 are adjusted by the percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap — the same mechanism used for First Round Picks.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) distinguishes between First Round Picks and all others: First Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by the percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap, while Rookie Scale Amounts for all other picks (including Second Round Picks) are increased by a flat 4%, not by the Salary Cap percentage change.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a player who is NOT a Developmental Player and whose Base Salary is exactly $500,001 is entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of her WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) provides that for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, only non-Developmental Players whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000 are entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment. A player earning $500,001 exceeds that threshold and is therefore not entitled to Team-provided housing under this provision.
