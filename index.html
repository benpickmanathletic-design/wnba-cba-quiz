Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), a Team currently has seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection. A player's Contract (which contains Base Salary protection) is suspended for the entire final Season pursuant to Article XIV, Section 6. Which of the following CORRECTLY states how that suspended Contract is treated with respect to the seven (7) Contract limit?
AA) The suspended Contract continues to count against the seven (7) Contract limit because the suspension does not eliminate the Base Salary protection already agreed upon.
BB) The suspended Contract does NOT count against the seven (7) Contract limit, meaning the Team could sign an eighth Contract containing Base Salary protection without exceeding the limit.
CC) The suspended Contract does NOT count against the seven (7) Contract limit only if the suspension is for a non-basketball reason.
DD) The suspended Contract counts as only one-half (0.5) of a Contract toward the seven (7) Contract limit for the Season in which the suspension occurs.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), a Player Contract of a player who has been suspended for the entire final Season of such Contract pursuant to Article XIV, Section 6 shall NOT count against the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection. This is an explicit carve-out that frees up one slot on the limit.
Q2Art. V Sec. 4(e)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick selected 'in the Lottery,' which of the following correctly describes the Base Salary protection status for the FIRST Season of such Contract under the 2026 CBA?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is NOT protected at signing; it becomes fully protected only if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the first Regular Season.
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season is FULLY PROTECTED upon signing the Contract, with no contingency required.
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the fourth-year option.
DD) The Base Salary for the first Season is protected for injury only; lack-of-skill protection does not attach until the start of the second Season.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(i), for a Lottery First Round Pick, the Base Salary for the FIRST Season of the Rookie Scale Contract 'shall be fully protected' automatically—there is no contingency or condition precedent unlike the protection triggers for non-Lottery picks. This distinguishes Lottery picks from all other Draft Rookies.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(g)(i)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), a Contract signed in connection with an agreement to trade it pursuant to Art. V, Sec. 3(f) also contains a trade bonus in Exhibit 8. Which of the following correctly describes when the trade bonus is payable?
AA) The trade bonus is payable upon the initial trade executed pursuant to the Sec. 3(f) agreement, because that trade triggers the bonus as the first trade of the Contract.
BB) The trade bonus is NOT payable upon the initial trade executed pursuant to the Sec. 3(f) agreement; it shall instead be payable only the SECOND time the Contract is traded.
CC) The trade bonus is payable upon the initial trade only if the player has at least six (6) Years of Service at the time the Contract was signed.
DD) The trade bonus is forfeited entirely when a Contract is signed in connection with a Sec. 3(f) trade agreement; no subsequent trade can trigger the bonus.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(i), when a Contract is signed in connection with an agreement to trade it under Sec. 3(f) and the Contract contains a trade bonus, the bonus shall NOT apply to that initial Sec. 3(f) trade but shall instead be payable only the SECOND time the Contract is traded. This is an explicit exception to the general rule that the bonus is payable only the first time a Contract is traded.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), which of the following MOST accurately describes the rule regarding Seasons played under a Core Player Contract Extension toward the maximum number of Core Player designations a Team may make?
AA) All Seasons played under any Core Player Contract—including Seasons during any Extension of that Contract—count toward the maximum number of Core Player designations.
BB) Only the Seasons played under the original Core Player Contract (while the player was designated as a Core Player) count toward the maximum; Seasons covered by any Extension of that Contract do NOT count.
CC) Seasons played under an Extension of a Core Player Contract count toward the maximum only if the Extension itself was signed while the player was still designated as a Core Player.
DD) No Seasons played under a Core Player Contract or its Extension count toward the maximum; only the act of designation itself is counted.
CBA Reference
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), any Veteran Free Agent who was previously designated as a Core Player may be counted toward the maximum, but only the Seasons played 'while such player was so designated (but not including Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract)' are included in the maximum count. Extensions are explicitly excluded from this tally.
Q5Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for a given Salary Cap Year arises when which of the following conditions is met?
AA) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams in that Season.
BB) The Salary Cap for a Salary Cap Year would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for the 2027 Salary Cap Year) but for the operation of the cap increase limit under Sec. 1(a)(ii).
CC) A Team's Team Salary falls below 85% of the Salary Cap at the conclusion of a Season.
DD) Total Benefits for a Salary Cap Year exceed Salary Cap Benefits for that year by more than five percent (5%).
CBA Reference
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' arises when the Salary Cap for a given Salary Cap Year would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for 2027) but for the operation of the cap-increase ceiling in Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the excess amount multiplied by the number of Teams. This is distinct from a 'Guarantee Shortfall,' which is triggered when Total Salaries fall short of the aggregate cap.
Q6Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2031 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year.
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year.
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year.
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii)(E), for each of the 2031 and 2032 Salary Cap Years, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the Salary Cap Year immediately preceding the applicable Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the Salary Cap Year immediately preceding the applicable Salary Cap Year. For 2031, that means 30.5% of 2030 League Revenue plus 13% of 2030 Team Revenue.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for non-Toronto Tempo players, which of the following correctly describes the in-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the out-of-pocket threshold at which in-network coverage reaches 100% under the 2026 CBA medical plan?
AA) $600 in-network annual deductible per covered individual; in-network coverage reaches 100% after the covered individual has paid $6,000 per person in a calendar year.
BB) $1,800 in-network annual deductible per covered individual (family rate applied to individuals); in-network coverage reaches 100% after $9,000 per person.
CC) $600 in-network annual deductible per covered individual; in-network coverage reaches 100% after the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year.
DD) $2,500 in-network annual deductible per covered individual; in-network coverage reaches 100% after the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person in a calendar year.
CBA Reference
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i), for non-Toronto Tempo players, in-network services carry a $600 annual deductible per covered individual and coverage at 80% until the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person ($9,000 per family) in a calendar year, after which coverage is 100%. The $2,500 deductible and $8,000 threshold apply to out-of-network services.
Q8Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, which of the following correctly states the per-Regular-Season-game maximum fine percentage AND the aggregate fine cap for missing Regular Season games in any Season under the 2026 CBA?
AA) Up to 5% of Base Salary per Regular Season game missed; aggregate cap of 25% of Base Salary for missing Regular Season games in any Season.
BB) Up to 2.5% of Base Salary per Regular Season game missed; aggregate cap of 20% of Base Salary for missing Regular Season games in any Season.
CC) Up to 2.5% of Base Salary per Regular Season game missed; aggregate cap of 25% of Base Salary for missing Regular Season games in any Season.
DD) Up to 5% of Base Salary per Regular Season game missed; aggregate cap of 20% of Base Salary for missing Regular Season games in any Season.
CBA Reference
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, a player may be fined up to 2½% of her Base Salary for each Regular Season game missed, with an aggregate cap of 20% of her Base Salary for missing Regular Season games in any Season. The 5% per-game rate and 25% aggregate cap apply specifically to playoff games, not Regular Season games.
Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a player who is NOT a Developmental Player and whose Base Salary is exactly $500,000 IS entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of her WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, all non-Developmental Players 'whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000' are entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment. Because the threshold is 'less than or equal to $500,000,' a player earning exactly $500,000 is included and therefore IS entitled to the housing benefit.
Q10Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), Rookie Scale Amounts applicable to Rookie Scale Contracts for Second Round Picks commencing with each Season after the 2026 Season are adjusted by the same percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap that governs First Round Pick adjustments, rather than by a fixed annual increase.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), First Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by the percentage change in the Salary Cap from year to year, while Second Round Pick (and Third Round Pick) Rookie Scale Amounts are instead increased by a fixed 4% annually regardless of Salary Cap movement. The two adjustment mechanisms are therefore different, making the statement false.
