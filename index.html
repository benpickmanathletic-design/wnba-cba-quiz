Q1Art. VII Sec. 1(a)4-choice
Under the 2026 CBA, what is the exact Salary Cap figure for the 2026 Salary Cap Year, and what is the maximum percentage by which the 2027 Salary Cap may increase or decrease from it? (Art. VII, Sec. 1(a))
AA) $6,000,000; capped at 10% change
BB) $7,000,000; capped at 13% change
CC) $7,000,000; capped at 10% change
DD) $8,000,000; capped at 13% change
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(i) sets the 2026 Salary Cap at $7,000,000. Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) specifies the 2027 cap may not increase or decrease by more than 13% from the 2026 cap, while all subsequent years are capped at 10% changes.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(b)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), what is the maximum number of Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection of any kind that a Team may have at one time, and which of the following correctly states an exception to that limit?
AA) Six (6); Rookie Scale Contracts for Lottery picks never count against the limit
BB) Seven (7); a player suspended for the entire final Season of her Contract under Art. XIV, Sec. 6 does not count against the limit
CC) Seven (7); any Contract with only conditional protection never counts against the limit
DD) Eight (8); Offer Sheets with Base Salary protection never count against the limit
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b) establishes a per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection. One stated exception is that the Contract of a player suspended for the entire final Season pursuant to Art. XIV, Sec. 6 or Art. XX, Sec. 1(c) does not count against that limit.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(i)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick selected 'in the Lottery,' when does the Base Salary for the second Season become fully protected?
AA) Immediately upon signing the Rookie Scale Contract
BB) If waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by the Contract
CC) If waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by the Contract
DD) Only if the Team exercises the fourth-year option
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(i) states that for a Lottery pick's Rookie Scale Contract, the Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by such Contract. By contrast, the first Season salary is fully protected from signing.
Q4Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), which of the following correctly describes a restriction on trade bonuses introduced in the 2026 CBA?
AA) A trade bonus may not exceed fifteen percent (15%) of the Base Salary remaining at the time of the trade
BB) No Contract signed by a player with fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus
CC) A trade bonus is payable each time a Contract is traded, up to a maximum of three times
DD) A trade bonus may only be added to a Contract at the time of signing, never through an Extension
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) explicitly states that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. Additionally, Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii) caps the trade bonus at ten percent (10%), not fifteen percent.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 7 / Art. I (Qualifying Offer definition)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7, regarding Core Player designations, which of the following is an accurate statement about the new 2026 CBA rules?
AA) Each Team may designate up to two (2) Core Players per Salary Cap Year
BB) A Core Qualifying Offer must be fully protected for lack of skill and injury or illness
CC) A player may be designated as a Core Player even if she has played pursuant to a Contract signed while she was a Core Player in a prior designation period
DD) The First Refusal Exercise Notice Deadline for a Core Player Offer Sheet is 72 hours after receipt
CBA Reference
Per Art. I (Definitions) and Art. VI, Sec. 7, a Core Qualifying Offer must be fully protected for lack of skill and injury or illness — this is the only type of Qualifying Offer required to carry full protection. Each Team is limited to one (1) Core Player designation, not two.

BB) Three (3) business days after receipt
CC) 5:00 p.m. (ET) on the fourth day following the date the Team receives the Offer Sheet, including weekends and holidays

Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), what is the in-network annual deductible per covered individual under the WNBA medical plan for non-Toronto Tempo players, and at what out-of-pocket threshold does coverage reach 100% in-network?
AA) $500 deductible; $2,500 out-of-pocket maximum
BB) $600 deductible; $3,000 out-of-pocket maximum per person
CC) $1,000 deductible; $5,000 out-of-pocket maximum
DD) $600 deductible; $9,000 out-of-pocket maximum per person
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) specifies a $600 annual in-network deductible per covered individual, with coverage at 80% until the individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year, after which coverage is 100% with no annual or lifetime maximum benefit.
Q8Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, what is the maximum aggregate fine that can be imposed on a player for missing Regular Season games in a single Season, and what is the per-game maximum fine percentage?
AA) Maximum 10% of Base Salary per Season; 2% per game
BB) Maximum 20% of Base Salary per Season; 2.5% per Regular Season game
CC) Maximum 25% of Base Salary per Season; 5% per Regular Season game
DD) Maximum 20% of Base Salary per Season; 5% per Regular Season game
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 states that a player may be fined up to 2.5% of Base Salary for each Regular Season game missed, and that aggregate fines for missing Regular Season games may not exceed 20% of the player's Base Salary in any Season.
Q9Art. VII Sec. 1(c)(i)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), the Minimum Team Salary for any Salary Cap Year is set at eighty percent (80%) of the Salary Cap for that year.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
This is FALSE. Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at eighty-five percent (85%) — not eighty percent — of the Salary Cap for the applicable Salary Cap Year.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), beginning with the 2029 Season all players (not Developmental Players) regardless of salary level are entitled to Team-provided one-bedroom apartments in their home city.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
This is FALSE. Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) specifies that for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, only players whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000 are entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment; players earning above that threshold are no longer guaranteed Team-provided housing.
