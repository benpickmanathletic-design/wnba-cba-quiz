Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is ELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA player with exactly five (5) Years of Service at the time of signing the Contract or Extension
BA player with exactly six (6) Years of Service at the time of signing the Contract or Extension
CA player with four (4) Years of Service who is a Restricted Free Agent signing an Offer Sheet
DA player with three (3) Years of Service who is re-signing with her Prior Team
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) expressly prohibits any Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service from containing a trade bonus. A player with exactly six (6) Years of Service meets the minimum threshold and is therefore eligible.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), the Rookie Scale Contract exception to the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts with Base Salary protection applies during which precise window and under what condition does it cease?
AIt applies for the first two Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and ceases once the Team exercises the Fourth Year Option
BIt applies during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and ceases upon the commencement of the extended term of any Extension of such Contract
CIt applies for the first three Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and ceases at the start of the Option Year
DIt applies throughout the entire term of a Rookie Scale Contract including any extended term, and only ceases upon termination of the Contract
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) states the exception applies 'with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract' and expressly provides it 'shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' It ceases upon commencement of the extended term of any Extension.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick who was NOT selected 'in the Lottery,' which of the following CORRECTLY describes the Base Salary protection rule for the FIRST Season of such Contract under the 2026 CBA?
AThe Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon execution of the Contract
BThe Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by the Contract
CThe Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the Fourth Year Option
DSuch a Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(ii) provides that for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by any First Round Pick other than a Lottery pick, the Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by the Contract. This distinguishes non-Lottery First Round Picks from Lottery picks, whose first Season is immediately fully protected.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays two (2) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated and then four (4) additional Seasons under an Extension of that Contract. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays two (2) Seasons under her Contract (no extension). How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AEight (8) Seasons total
BSix (6) Seasons total
CFour (4) Seasons total
DFive (5) Seasons total
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) specifies that only Seasons played under the Contract signed while designated — 'but not including Seasons covered by any Extension' — count toward the Team's Core Player designation limit. Player A's two (2) Seasons under the original Contract count; her four (4) Extension Seasons do not. Player B's two (2) Seasons under her original Contract count. Total: 2 + 2 = 4 Seasons.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b); Art. I (Qualifying Offer)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) and Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Veteran Free Agent with exactly eight (8) Years of Service receives a Restricted Qualifying Offer from her Prior Team. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AThe Standard Maximum Salary applicable to the upcoming Season
B105% of the player's Base Salary from the final Season of her prior Contract
CThe greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
DThe Supermax Salary applicable to the upcoming Season
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' subsection (D) provides that for a player subject to a Right of First Refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), a Restricted Qualifying Offer must equal the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract. Eight Years of Service falls within Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)'s scope (four or more Years of Service).
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii), the 'Guarantee Shortfall' for a given Salary Cap Year is triggered and computed under which of the following conditions?
AWhen the Salary Cap for a Salary Cap Year would have increased by more than 10% but for the cap on increases, the shortfall equals the excess amount multiplied by the number of Teams
BWhen, as of the conclusion of a Season, Total Salaries for that Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap for that Year multiplied by the total number of Teams, the shortfall equals that difference
CWhen a Team's Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary, the shortfall equals 85% of the Salary Cap minus actual Team Salary
DWhen the WNBA fails to distribute League Revenue equally among Teams, the shortfall equals the underpaid amount per Team
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) defines the 'Guarantee Shortfall' as the amount by which Total Salaries for a Salary Cap Year fall short of the Salary Cap for that Year multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in that Season. This is distinct from the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall in Sec. 1(b)(i), which addresses cap increase restrictions.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states the out-of-network deductible per covered individual AND the percentage of most eligible out-of-network charges covered BEFORE the out-of-pocket threshold is reached?
A$2,500 deductible; 70% coverage of most eligible charges before reaching the $8,000 out-of-pocket threshold
B$6,250 deductible; 60% coverage of most eligible charges before reaching the $20,000 out-of-pocket threshold
C$2,500 deductible; 60% coverage of most eligible charges before reaching the $8,000 out-of-pocket threshold
D$1,800 deductible; 80% coverage of most eligible charges before reaching the $9,000 out-of-pocket threshold
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) states that for non-Toronto Tempo players, out-of-network services carry a $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual ($6,250 per family) and coverage for sixty percent (60%) of most eligible charges until the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person in a calendar year, after which coverage reaches 100%.
Q8Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2032 Salary Cap Year?
A30.5% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year
B30.25% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year
C30.5% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year
D30.5% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) provides that for each of the 2031 and 2032 Salary Cap Years, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year. For the 2032 Salary Cap Year, the immediately preceding year is 2031.
Q9Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, the 20% aggregate Regular Season fine cap expressly does NOT limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed for the same missed Regular Season games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 expressly states that 'nothing in this Section 7 shall be construed to limit a Team's right... to impose a suspension or other discipline on a player for the same (or other) missed games in accordance with this Agreement' and that '(the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension).' Therefore, the statement is True.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a non-Developmental Player whose Base Salary is exactly $600,000 is entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of her WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) limits the one-bedroom apartment entitlement for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons to non-Developmental Players whose Base Salary is 'less than or equal to $500,000.' A player earning $600,000 exceeds that threshold and is therefore not entitled to Team-provided housing under this provision.
