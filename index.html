Q1Art. V Sec. 4(e)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a non-Lottery First Round Pick, which of the following correctly describes when the Base Salary for the FIRST Season becomes fully protected?
AA) It is fully protected upon signing of the Contract, with no condition.
BB) It becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CC) It becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the option for the fourth Season.
DD) It becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(ii), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by any First Round Pick not selected in the Lottery, the Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract. This contrasts with Lottery picks, whose first Season is fully protected upon signing.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(ii) and (vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), what is the maximum permissible trade bonus amount as a percentage of Base Salary remaining at the time of the trade, and which of the following statements about trade bonus eligibility is CORRECT under the 2026 CBA?
AA) Maximum 15%; any player with four or more Years of Service may include a trade bonus in a new Contract.
BB) Maximum 10%; no Contract or Extension entered into by a player with fewer than six (6) Years of Service at the time of signing may contain a trade bonus.
CC) Maximum 10%; any player with five or more Years of Service may include a trade bonus in a new Contract or Extension.
DD) Maximum 15%; no Contract or Extension entered into by a player with fewer than five (5) Years of Service at the time of signing may contain a trade bonus.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii) states a trade bonus shall not exceed ten percent (10%) of the Base Salary remaining at the time of the trade. Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) expressly prohibits any Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service from containing a trade bonus.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), which of the following correctly describes the treatment of a Rookie Scale Contract that contains Base Salary protection with respect to the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection?
AA) A Rookie Scale Contract with Base Salary protection counts against the seven (7) Contract limit for all four Seasons of the Contract, including any extended term.
BB) A Rookie Scale Contract with Base Salary protection never counts against the seven (7) Contract limit, regardless of whether the Contract has been extended.
CC) A Rookie Scale Contract with Base Salary protection does not count against the seven (7) Contract limit during the four Seasons of the Contract, but this exception does not apply during the extended term of any such Contract that has been extended.
DD) A Rookie Scale Contract with Base Salary protection counts against the seven (7) Contract limit only during the Option Year (fourth Season).
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) provides that any Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection shall not count against the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts with Base Salary protection, but this exception only applies during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract and does not apply to the extended term of any such Contract that has been extended.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent is eligible to receive a Restricted Qualifying Offer—and thereby become a Restricted Free Agent—if she has how many Years of Service, and what is the baseline salary floor for such an offer?
AA) Three (3) Years of Service; the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or 105% of the prior Contract's last Season Base Salary.
BB) Four (4) Years of Service; the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
CC) Five (5) Years of Service; the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
DD) Four (4) Years of Service; 105% of the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) provides that any Veteran Free Agent who has four (4) Years of Service is eligible to receive a Restricted Qualifying Offer. Per Art. I (definition of 'Qualifying Offer'), a Restricted Qualifying Offer must be the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
Q5Art. VII Sec. 1(c)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), the Minimum Team Salary for a Salary Cap Year equals what percentage of the Salary Cap, and what is the WNBA's obligation if a Team falls short of this threshold at the conclusion of a Season?
AA) 80%; the WNBA shall cause the Team to make up the shortfall to the Players Association.
BB) 85%; the WNBA shall cause the Team to make payments equal to the shortfall, disbursed to players on that Team proportionally or per WNBPA formula.
CC) 85%; the WNBA shall fine the Team but is not required to disburse funds to players.
DD) 80%; the WNBA shall cause the Team to make payments equal to the shortfall, disbursed equally to all WNBA players that Season.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap for that Salary Cap Year. Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(ii) requires the WNBA to cause any deficient Team to make payments equal to the shortfall, to be disbursed to players on such Team either proportionally to Base Salaries or per a formula reasonably determined by the Players Association.
Q6Art. VIII Sec. (c)4-choice
Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), how are Rookie Scale Amounts for Second Round Picks in Seasons after the 2026 Season adjusted, and how does this differ from the adjustment rule for First Round Picks?
AA) Both First Round and Second Round Picks have their Rookie Scale Amounts increased by 4% each Season.
BB) First Round Picks' Rookie Scale Amounts increase by 4% annually; Second Round Picks' amounts increase by the same percentage as the Salary Cap increase.
CC) First Round Picks' Rookie Scale Amounts increase or decrease by the same percentage as the Salary Cap; Second Round Picks' amounts increase by a fixed 4% annually.
DD) Both First Round and Second Round Picks have their Rookie Scale Amounts adjusted by the percentage change in the Salary Cap.
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) provides that for First Round Picks, Rookie Scale Amounts for subsequent Seasons increase or decrease by the percentage change in the Salary Cap. For Second Round Picks (and Third Round Picks/All Others), the Rookie Scale Amounts increase by a fixed 4% annually regardless of Salary Cap movement.
Q7Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, which of the following correctly states both the per-Regular-Season-game maximum fine percentage AND the maximum aggregate fine cap for missing Regular Season games in a Season?
AA) Up to 5% per game missed; aggregate cap of 20% of Base Salary.
BB) Up to 2.5% per game missed; aggregate cap of 25% of Base Salary.
CC) Up to 2.5% per game missed; aggregate cap of 20% of Base Salary.
DD) Up to 5% per game missed; aggregate cap of 25% of Base Salary.
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 provides that a Team or the WNBA may fine a player up to two and one-half percent (2.5%) of her Base Salary for each Regular Season game missed, with a maximum aggregate fine of twenty percent (20%) of her Base Salary for missing Regular Season games in any Season. The 5% per-game and 25% aggregate caps apply only to playoff games.
Q8Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for non-Toronto Tempo players, which of the following correctly states the in-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the out-of-pocket threshold at which in-network coverage reaches 100%?
AA) $600 annual deductible; 100% coverage after $8,000 out-of-pocket per person.
BB) $2,500 annual deductible; 100% coverage after $3,000 out-of-pocket per person.
CC) $600 annual deductible; 100% coverage after $3,000 out-of-pocket per person.
DD) $1,800 annual deductible; 100% coverage after $9,000 out-of-pocket per family.
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) specifies that non-Toronto Tempo players receive in-network services with a $600 annual deductible per covered individual and coverage at 80% until the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year, after which coverage reaches 100%. The $2,500 deductible and $8,000 threshold apply to out-of-network services.
Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, ALL players who are not Developmental Players are entitled to Team-provided one-bedroom apartments regardless of their Base Salary level.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, only players (who are not Developmental Players) whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000 during the corresponding Season are entitled to Team-provided one-bedroom apartments. Players earning more than $500,000 are NOT automatically entitled to Team-provided housing in those Seasons.
Q10Art. VI Sec. 7(b)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(b), a player who has previously played pursuant to a Contract signed while she was designated as a Core Player by a Team is ineligible to be designated as a Core Player by that same Team during the Core Player Designation period prior to the 2026 Season.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(b) states that no player may be designated as a Core Player during the designation period prior to the 2026 Season if such player has played pursuant to a Contract signed while she was designated as a Core Player by such Team. Additionally, Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) limits each Team to no more than one Core Player designation at a time.
