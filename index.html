Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), a player's Contract is suspended for the entire final Season pursuant to Article XIV, Section 6. Which of the following correctly states how that Contract is treated with respect to the seven (7) Player Contract limit for Base Salary protection?
AA) It counts against the limit because the player remains on the roster.
BB) It does not count against the limit, provided the suspension covers the entire final Season of such Contract.
CC) It counts against the limit unless the Team waives the player within 30 days of the suspension.
DD) It does not count against the limit only if the Contract contains no skill protection, only injury protection.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), a Contract is explicitly excluded from the seven-Contract limit if the player has been suspended for the entire final Season of such Contract pursuant to Article XIV, Section 6 or Article XX, Section 1(c). The exclusion applies regardless of the type of protection in the Contract.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), a player has five (5) Years of Service at the time she signs an Extension. Which of the following statements about trade bonus eligibility for that Extension is CORRECT under the 2026 CBA?
AA) The Extension may contain a trade bonus because five Years of Service satisfies any service threshold.
BB) The Extension may not contain a trade bonus because a player needs at least six (6) Years of Service at the time of signing to include a trade bonus.
CC) The Extension may contain a trade bonus only if the underlying Contract already contained a trade bonus.
DD) The Extension may contain a trade bonus only if the player's Base Salary exceeds the Standard Maximum Salary.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) explicitly states that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. Five Years of Service falls short of this threshold.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(i)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Lottery First Round Pick's Rookie Scale Contract, when does the Base Salary for the THIRD Season become fully protected?
AA) Automatically upon execution of the Contract.
BB) If waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the third Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CC) Only if the option for the fourth Season is exercised by the Team.
DD) If the player is named to an All-WNBA team in either of the first two Seasons.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(i), for a Lottery pick's Rookie Scale Contract, the Base Salary for the third and fourth Seasons both become fully protected only if the Team exercises its option for the fourth Season. This is a critical distinction from the treatment of the first two Seasons.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), which of the following correctly describes the treatment of Seasons played under an Extension of a Core Player Contract toward the maximum number of Core Player designations a Team may make?
AA) All Seasons played under both the original Core Player Contract and any Extension thereof count toward the maximum.
BB) Seasons played under a Core Player Contract count toward the maximum, but Seasons covered by any Extension of that Contract do NOT count.
CC) Only the first Season of a Core Player Contract counts toward the maximum, regardless of Extensions.
DD) Extensions count toward the maximum only if the Extension was signed before the player's third Season under the Core Player Contract.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) states that only Seasons played while a player was designated as a Core Player (but not including Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract) shall be included in the maximum count. Extensions are expressly carved out from this tally.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service is eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer. Which of the following correctly states the salary floor for such an offer?
AA) The Supermax Salary applicable to the upcoming Season.
BB) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
CC) One hundred five percent (105%) of the player's fourth-year Base Salary.
DD) The Standard Maximum Salary applicable to the upcoming Season.
CBA Reference
Under Art. I (the definition of 'Qualifying Offer') and Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Restricted Qualifying Offer for a player eligible under Sec. 5(b) must equal the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of the prior Contract. This is designated a 'Restricted Qualifying Offer' in the definitions.
Q6Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2028 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year.
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year.
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year.
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) specifies that for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year. The 12.25% Team Revenue rate applies to the 2029 Salary Cap Year, not 2028.
Q7Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Guarantee Shortfall' arises when which of the following conditions is met at the conclusion of a Season?
AA) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year exceed the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams.
BB) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in such Season.
CC) A single Team's Team Salary falls below 85% of the Salary Cap.
DD) The Salary Cap for the year increases by more than 13% from the prior year.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) defines the Guarantee Shortfall as the amount by which Total Salaries for a Salary Cap Year falls short of the Salary Cap for that year multiplied by the total number of Teams in the WNBA that Season. This is a league-wide shortfall, distinct from the per-Team Minimum Team Salary obligation.
Q8Art. XI Sec. 1(a)4-choice
Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a player whose Base Salary is $501,000 is NOT entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment. Which provision, also in Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), addresses housing for a player in this situation whose Contract is assigned from one Team to another during the Season?
AA) The assignee Team must provide a one-bedroom apartment for the full remainder of the Season.
BB) The assignee Team must provide an individual hotel room in the assignee Team's home city for up to thirty-five (35) days following the assignment.
CC) The assignor Team remains responsible for housing costs through the end of the Season.
DD) The player receives a $5,000 relocation stipend but no Team-provided housing of any kind.
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a) states that for any player not entitled to Team-provided housing whose Contract is assigned, the assignee Team must provide an individual hotel room in its home city for up to 35 days following the assignment. This provision applies specifically to players above the $500,000 Base Salary threshold in 2029 and 2030.
Q9Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv)T/F
True or False: Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), a Contract that does NOT already contain a trade bonus may be amended to add a trade bonus at any time, provided the player has at least six (6) Years of Service.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv) provides that a Contract without a trade bonus generally cannot be amended to add one, with a narrow exception: if the Contract is extended, it may be amended simultaneously with the Extension to provide a trade bonus. Mere passage of time or accumulation of Years of Service does not allow a standalone amendment to add a trade bonus.
Q10Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), Rookie Scale Amounts for Third Round Picks in Seasons after 2026 are adjusted by the same fixed 4% annual increase that applies to Second Round Picks, rather than by the percentage change in the Salary Cap.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) distinguishes between First Round Picks, whose Rookie Scale Amounts track the percentage change in the Salary Cap, and all other picks (including Second and Third Round Picks), whose amounts increase by a fixed 4% per Season after 2026. Third Round Picks follow the same fixed 4% rule as Second Round Picks.
