Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), which specific category of suspended player is expressly carved out of the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection, such that her Contract does NOT count against that limit?
AA) A player suspended by the WNBA for a violation of Article XIV, Section 7 (missing games)
BB) A player whose Contract has been suspended by her Team for the first half of the Season pursuant to Article XIV, Section 9
CC) A player who has been suspended for the entire final Season of her Player Contract pursuant to Article XIV, Section 6 or Article XX, Section 1(c)
DD) A player suspended indefinitely by the Commissioner under Article XIV, Section 5
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(ii) expressly carves out 'the Player Contract of a player who has been suspended for the entire final Season of such Player Contract pursuant to Article XIV, Section 6 or Article XX, Section 1(c)' from the seven-Contract limit. No other suspension category receives this carve-out.
Q2Art. V Sec. 4(e)(i)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick selected 'in the Lottery,' which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rule for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is NOT protected in any form; it only becomes protected if waivers are not requested before the first day of the first Regular Season
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season shall be fully protected immediately upon signing, with no condition precedent required
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the fourth-year option
DD) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the second Regular Season
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(i) states that for a Lottery pick, '(1) the Base Salary for the first Season of such Contract shall be fully protected' — there is no triggering condition stated, unlike non-Lottery First Round Picks whose first-Season protection is conditional on waivers not being requested before the first day of the first Regular Season.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is ELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player who, at signing, has exactly five (5) Years of Service
BB) A player who, at signing, has exactly four (4) Years of Service
CC) A player who, at signing, has exactly six (6) Years of Service
DD) A player who, at signing, has exactly three (3) Years of Service
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) provides that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. Therefore only a player with at least six (6) Years of Service at the time of signing is eligible.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays one (1) Season under the Contract signed while designated and then two (2) additional Seasons under an Extension of that Contract. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays three (3) Seasons under her Contract (no extension). How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) 6 Seasons
BB) 5 Seasons
CC) 4 Seasons
DD) 3 Seasons
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that only Seasons played under the Contract signed while designated (but NOT Seasons covered by any Extension) count toward the limit. Player A contributes 1 Season (Extension Seasons are excluded); Player B contributes 3 Seasons — for a total of 4 Seasons.
Q5Art. I (def. 'Qualifying Offer'); Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) and Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Veteran Free Agent with exactly seven (7) Years of Service receives a Restricted Qualifying Offer from her Prior Team. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) 105% of the player's fourth year Rookie Scale Base Salary
BB) The applicable Standard Maximum Salary for the upcoming Season
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
DD) The applicable Supermax Salary for the upcoming Season
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' clause (D) states that for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), the Restricted Qualifying Offer must equal 'the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.' Years of Service beyond the threshold (four or more) do not change this floor.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for a Salary Cap Year arises when which condition is met, and how is the shortfall amount computed?
AA) It arises when Total Salaries fall below the Salary Cap multiplied by the number of Teams; the shortfall equals that deficit
BB) It arises when the Salary Cap for a given year would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for 2027) but for the cap on increases; the shortfall equals the excess amount that was capped, multiplied by the number of Teams
CC) It arises when any single Team's Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary; the shortfall equals the per-team deficit summed across all Teams
DD) It arises when the WNBA fails to distribute Guarantee Shortfall payments within 60 days; the shortfall equals the unpaid amount plus interest
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) defines the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall as arising when the Salary Cap 'would have increased by more than ten percent (10%) (or thirteen percent (13%) for 2027) but for' the cap-increase limit; the shortfall equals the additional amount by which the cap would have increased beyond those thresholds, multiplied by the total number of Teams.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states the in-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the percentage of eligible charges covered AFTER the deductible but BEFORE the in-network out-of-pocket threshold is reached?
AA) $600 deductible; 90% coverage until the out-of-pocket threshold
BB) $1,800 deductible; 80% coverage until the out-of-pocket threshold
CC) $600 deductible; 80% coverage until the out-of-pocket threshold
DD) $2,500 deductible; 60% coverage until the out-of-pocket threshold
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) specifies a '$600 annual deductible per covered individual' for in-network services, with 'coverage at eighty percent (80%) until the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year' — after which coverage reaches 100%.
Q8Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2033 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year
BB) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2033 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2033 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) states that for 'each of the 2031 and 2032 Salary Cap Years,' the formula is 30.5% of League Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year. The 2033 Salary Cap Year is not expressly enumerated but follows the same pattern established for 2031 and 2032, using 2032 revenues; the CBA expressly applies this formula for 2031 and 2032, and the 2033 cap year falls within the CBA term using the same formula.
Q9Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, the 20% aggregate Regular Season fine cap and the 25% aggregate playoff fine cap expressly DO apply to limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed for the same missed games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 expressly states: 'the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension.' The fine caps limit only the Section 7 per-game fines themselves, not salary reductions arising from separately imposed suspensions for the same missed games.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2031 Season and beyond, the CBA expressly entitles ALL non-Developmental Players, regardless of Base Salary level, to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of their WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a) provides Team-provided housing for all non-Developmental Players unconditionally only for the 2026, 2027, and 2028 Seasons; for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons it is limited to players with Base Salary at or below $500,000. The CBA does not expressly extend this entitlement to the 2031 Season or beyond in Art. XI, Sec. 1(a).
