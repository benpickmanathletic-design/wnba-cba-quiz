Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), which of the following players is PROHIBITED from having a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player with exactly six (6) Years of Service at the time of signing
BB) A player with five (5) Years of Service at the time of signing
CC) A player with seven (7) Years of Service at the time of signing
DD) A player with eight (8) Years of Service at the time of signing
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. A player with exactly five (5) Years of Service falls below that threshold and is therefore prohibited.
Q2Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick, which of the following correctly describes the Base Salary protection rules for the first Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the first Regular Season
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon execution of the Contract
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind
DD) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes protected only if the Team exercises the fourth-year option
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii) expressly states that a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season of such Contract. Protection for the second Season only arises if waivers are not requested before the first day of that second Regular Season.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(b)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), which of the following scenarios correctly describes an exception to the seven (7) Player Contract limit for Base Salary protection under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A Rookie Scale Contract extended beyond its initial four Seasons does not count against the limit during the extended term
BB) A Rookie Scale Contract (within its initial four Seasons) that contains Base Salary protection does not count against the limit
CC) An outstanding Offer Sheet providing for Base Salary protection does not count against the limit
DD) A player on a Rest-of-Season Contract with Base Salary protection does not count against the limit
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b) provides that any Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection (including if Base Salary was renegotiated) shall not count against the per-team limit of seven (7) protected contracts, but only during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract itself—not during any extended term. Outstanding Offer Sheets do count against the limit, making option C incorrect.
Q4Art. VII Sec. 1(c)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), what is the Minimum Team Salary as a percentage of the Salary Cap for any Salary Cap Year under the 2026 CBA?
AA) 80% of the Salary Cap
BB) 82.5% of the Salary Cap
CC) 85% of the Salary Cap
DD) 90% of the Salary Cap
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) explicitly states that the Minimum Team Salary for a Salary Cap Year shall equal eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap for such Salary Cap Year. This represents a change from the prior CBA, which set the floor at 80%.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 7(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(b), which of the following players is INELIGIBLE to be designated as a Core Player during the Core Player Designation period prior to the 2026 Season?
AA) An Unrestricted Free Agent who has never previously been designated as a Core Player
BB) A player who has played pursuant to a Contract signed while she was previously designated as a Core Player by the same Team
CC) A Restricted Free Agent who qualifies under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)
DD) A Veteran Free Agent with five (5) Years of Service who was never previously made a Core Player
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(b) states that no player may be designated as a Core Player prior to the 2026 Season if such player has played pursuant to a Contract signed while that player was previously designated as a Core Player by that Team (under Art. V, Sec. 12). This restriction is specific to the 2026 pre-season designation window.
Q6Art. VI Sec. 5(b); Art. I (Qualifying Offer definition)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent is eligible to receive a Restricted Qualifying Offer (and thereby become a Restricted Free Agent) if she has how many Years of Service, and what is the baseline salary floor for such a Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) Three (3) or more Years of Service; the applicable Minimum Player Salary
BB) Four (4) or more Years of Service; the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract
CC) Four (4) or more Years of Service; 105% of the Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract
DD) Five (5) or more Years of Service; the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) provides that a Veteran Free Agent with four (4) Years of Service may be made a Restricted Free Agent via a Qualifying Offer. Art. I (definition of 'Qualifying Offer') specifies that for such a player, the Restricted Qualifying Offer must be the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), what is the out-of-network annual deductible per covered individual under the WNBA medical plan for non-Toronto Tempo players, and at what out-of-pocket threshold does out-of-network coverage reach 100%?
AA) $2,000 deductible; 100% after $6,000 out-of-pocket
BB) $2,500 deductible; 100% after $8,000 out-of-pocket
CC) $2,500 deductible; 100% after $9,000 out-of-pocket
DD) $1,800 deductible; 100% after $8,000 out-of-pocket
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) states that for out-of-network services, there is a $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual ($6,250 per family), and coverage reaches one hundred percent (100%) after the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person ($20,000 per family) in a calendar year.
Q8Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, which of the following correctly states both the per-playoff-game maximum fine percentage and the maximum aggregate fine cap for missing playoff games in a Season?
AA) Up to 2.5% per playoff game; aggregate cap of 20% of Base Salary
BB) Up to 5% per playoff game; aggregate cap of 20% of Base Salary
CC) Up to 5% per playoff game; aggregate cap of 25% of Base Salary
DD) Up to 10% per playoff game; aggregate cap of 25% of Base Salary
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 provides that a Team or the WNBA may fine a player up to five percent (5%) of her Base Salary for each playoff game missed, and in no event may the aggregate fines for missing playoff games exceed twenty-five percent (25%) of the player's Base Salary in any Season.
Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, ALL players (who are not Developmental Players) are entitled to Team-provided one-bedroom apartments in their home city regardless of their Base Salary level.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
This is FALSE. Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) limits the Team-provided one-bedroom apartment entitlement for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons to players whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000 during the corresponding Season. Players earning more than $500,000 are not entitled to Team-provided housing in those Seasons.
Q10Art. VIII Sec. (b), (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, the Rookie Scale Amount for the first Season of a Rookie Scale Contract for the overall first pick in the 2026 WNBA Draft is $500,000, and Rookie Scale Amounts for Second Round Picks in subsequent Seasons increase by the same percentage as the Salary Cap increase rather than a fixed 4% rate.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
This is FALSE. While Art. VIII correctly sets the first-year Rookie Scale Amount for the first overall pick at $500,000 for 2026, Art. VIII, Sec. (c) provides that for Seasons after 2026, Second Round (and Third Round) Pick Rookie Scale Amounts increase by a fixed four percent (4%), not by the Salary Cap percentage increase (which applies only to First Round Picks).
