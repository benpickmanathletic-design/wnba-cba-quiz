Q1Art. VII §1(a)4-choice
Under Article VII, what is the 2026 Salary Cap amount established by the 2026-2033 CBA?
AA) $5,000,000
BB) $6,000,000
CC) $7,000,000
DD) $8,000,000
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Section 1(a) explicitly states: 'The Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year shall be $7,000,000.' This was a significant increase from the prior CBA and represents a landmark change in the 2026 agreement.
Q2Art. VII §1(a)4-choice
Under Article VII, Section 1(a), by what maximum percentage can the Salary Cap increase or decrease for the 2027 Salary Cap Year relative to 2026?
AA) 10%
BB) 11%
CC) 12%
DD) 13%
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Section 1(a) creates a special carve-out for 2027: the Salary Cap 'shall not increase or decrease by more than thirteen percent (13%) from the Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year.' For all subsequent years after 2027, the limit is 10%.
Q3Art. VII §1(b)4-choice
Under Article VII, the 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for a given Salary Cap Year is triggered when the cap would have increased beyond the applicable percentage limit. Which formula correctly describes how the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall is calculated?
AA) The excess amount above the cap limit multiplied by total number of Teams
BB) The total Salary Cap for that year minus Total Salaries paid
CC) The Designated 20% Share of SBR minus Total Salaries paid
DD) The difference between the prior year cap and the current year cap
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Section 1(b) defines the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall as 'the additional amount by which the Salary Cap would have increased but for the operation of Section 1(a)(ii)...multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in the Season covered by such Salary Cap Year.'
Q4Art. V §34-choice
Under Article V, Section 3, which of the following parties is PROHIBITED from negotiating allowable amendments to a Standard Player Contract?
AA) A Veteran Player entering her second WNBA season
BB) A Veteran Player who has played five WNBA seasons
CC) A Rookie entering her first WNBA season
DD) A player who has previously signed a multi-year contract
CBA Reference
Art. V, Section 3 explicitly states: 'In their individual contract negotiations, a Veteran Player (but not a Rookie) and a Team may amend the provisions of a Standard Player Contract.' Rookies are expressly excluded from negotiating allowable amendments.
Q5Art. V §3(b)4-choice
Under Article V, Section 3(b), which of the following is NOT listed as a protected reason for which Base Salary may be protected in the event of contract termination?
AA) Lack of skill
BB) Basketball-related injury
CC) In-Season injury or illness
DD) Voluntary retirement by the player
CBA Reference
Art. V, Section 3(b) lists the following protected categories: (i) lack of skill; (ii) disability or unfitness from basketball-related injury; (iii) disability or unfitness from in-Season injury or illness. Voluntary retirement is not listed as a permissible protection category.
Q6Art. V §2(d)4-choice
Under Article V, Section 2(d), a player may unilaterally change the Standard Player Contract form in one specific way without mutual agreement. What is that permissible unilateral change?
AA) Adding a no-trade clause to the contract
BB) Deleting paragraph 6(d) of the Standard Player Contract in its entirety
CC) Modifying the Base Salary schedule in Exhibit 1
DD) Adding a performance bonus clause not otherwise permitted
CBA Reference
Art. V, Section 2(d) states: 'Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Section 2, a player may unilaterally change the form of the Standard Player Contract by deleting paragraph 6(d) thereof in its entirety.' No other unilateral change is permitted.
Q7Art. VI §1(a)4-choice
Under Article VI, Section 1(a), which free agent classification is the ONLY one that does NOT have the ability to negotiate with any team other than her Prior Team?
AA) Unrestricted Free Agent
BB) Restricted Free Agent
CC) Core Player
DD) Both Reserved Player and Core Player
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Section 1(a) shows that Unrestricted Free Agents may negotiate with any team, and Restricted Free Agents may negotiate Offer Sheets with any team other than Prior Team. Both Reserved Players and Core Players are limited to negotiating only with their Prior Team.
Q8Art. VI §1(b)4-choice
Under Article VI, Section 1(b), which of the following statements about compensation obligations and Rights of First Refusal for Free Agents is accurate?
AA) All Free Agents are subject to Right of First Refusal by their Prior Team
BB) No compensation obligation applies to any Free Agent, and no Right of First Refusal applies to any Free Agent other than a Restricted Free Agent
CC) Compensation obligations apply to Core Players but not Unrestricted Free Agents
DD) Rights of First Refusal may be individually negotiated into any Player Contract
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Section 1(b) explicitly provides: 'No compensation obligation of any kind to another Team shall be applicable to any Free Agent. No right of first refusal shall be applicable to any Free Agent other than a Restricted Free Agent.' This is a clean and absolute rule.
Q9Art. XIV §1T/F
True or False: Under Article XIV, Section 1, a player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another Team is explicitly stated to be in violation of the player conduct obligations set forth in that section.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Section 1 states: 'For avoidance of doubt, any player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another Team shall be in violation of this Section 1.' This is a notable and specific provision in the 2026 CBA's conduct standards.
Q10Art. X §1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Article X, Section 1, a player whose Standard Player Contract is terminated AFTER the Mid-Point of the Regular Season continues to receive medical benefits for only 30 days following contract termination.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. X, Section 1(a) creates a distinction: a player terminated BEFORE the Mid-Point receives benefits for 30 days post-termination. A player terminated AFTER the Mid-Point receives medical benefits 'until the end of the calendar year,' which is a significantly more generous protection.
