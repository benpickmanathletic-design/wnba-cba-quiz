Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), which of the following MOST ACCURATELY describes the scope of the Rookie Scale Contract exception to the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts with Base Salary protection — specifically, during which period does the exception apply AND when does it definitively cease?
AA) The exception applies for the entire life of the Rookie Scale Contract, including any extended term, and ceases only when the player's total Years of Service exceed four (4).
BB) The exception applies only during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract itself and ceases with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.
CC) The exception applies only during the first two Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract and ceases once the Team exercises the Fourth Year Option.
DD) The exception applies during the four Seasons and the extended term of the Rookie Scale Contract, but ceases if the Base Salary is renegotiated pursuant to Article VII, Section 5(c)(ii).
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) expressly states that the exception 'shall only apply with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' Once the contract is extended, it counts against the seven-contract cap.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is INELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A Veteran player who, at the time of signing, has exactly six (6) Years of Service.
BB) A Veteran player who, at the time of signing, has seven (7) Years of Service.
CC) A Veteran player who, at the time of signing, has five (5) Years of Service.
DD) A Veteran player who, at the time of signing, has exactly eight (8) Years of Service.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) provides that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. A player with five (5) Years of Service has fewer than six and is therefore ineligible.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick who was NOT selected 'in the Lottery,' which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rule for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected from the date of signing with no conditions.
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises its Fourth Year Option.
DD) Such a Rookie Scale Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(ii), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by any other First Round Pick (i.e., not a Lottery pick), the Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays five (5) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated and zero (0) Seasons under any Extension. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays two (2) Seasons under her Contract and two (2) Seasons under an Extension of that Contract. How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) Nine (9) Seasons total — five (5) for Player A and four (4) for Player B.
BB) Seven (7) Seasons total — five (5) for Player A and two (2) for Player B (Extension Seasons excluded).
CC) Four (4) Seasons total — zero (0) for Player A (no Extension) and four (4) for Player B.
DD) Two (2) Seasons total — only the non-Extension Seasons for Player B count.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that the Seasons played under the Contract signed while designated — but NOT Seasons covered by any Extension — count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit. Player A contributes five (5) non-Extension Seasons; Player B contributes only the two (2) Seasons under her original Contract, not the two (2) Extension Seasons, for a total of seven (7).
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b); Art. I (Qualifying Offer)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) and Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service is eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer from her Prior Team. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) 105% of the player's Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract.
BB) The Standard Maximum Salary for the applicable Salary Cap Year.
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
DD) The Maximum Player Salary pursuant to Article V, Section 8(c).
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' at subsection (D) states that for a player subject to a right of first refusal in accordance with Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), a Restricted Qualifying Offer must equal the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for the 2027 Salary Cap Year is triggered only if which specific condition is met, and how is the shortfall amount computed?
AA) It is triggered if the Salary Cap for the 2027 Year would have increased by more than 10% from the 2026 cap but for the cap on increases; the shortfall equals the excess amount multiplied by the number of Teams.
BB) It is triggered if Total Salaries for the 2027 Year are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the number of Teams; the shortfall equals that difference.
CC) It is triggered if the Salary Cap for the 2027 Year would have increased by more than 13% from the 2026 cap but for the operation of Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the additional amount above the 13% threshold multiplied by the total number of Teams.
DD) It is triggered if any Team's Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary; the shortfall equals the aggregate of all per-team deficits.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) states that the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall arises if the Salary Cap would have increased by more than 13% from the 2026 cap (for 2027) but for the operation of the cap limit rule; the shortfall equals the excess beyond the 13% increase multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA for that Season.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states BOTH the in-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the in-network out-of-pocket threshold at which coverage reaches 100%?
AA) $600 annual deductible per covered individual; 100% coverage after the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person in a calendar year.
BB) $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual; 100% coverage after the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year.
CC) $600 annual deductible per covered individual; 100% coverage after the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year.
DD) $1,800 annual deductible per covered individual; 100% coverage after the covered individual has paid $9,000 per family in a calendar year.
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) provides that in-network services carry a $600 annual deductible per covered individual ($1,800 per family), with coverage at 80% until the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person ($9,000 per family) in a calendar year, and 100% thereafter.

Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2031 Season and beyond, the CBA expressly entitles all non-Developmental Players, regardless of Base Salary level, to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of their WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a) provides Team-provided one-bedroom apartments for all non-Developmental Players only in the 2026, 2027, and 2028 Seasons (subsection ii), and only for those earning $500,000 or less in the 2029 and 2030 Seasons (subsection iii). The CBA contains no such express entitlement for 2031 or beyond.
Q10Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, if the WNBA or a Team imposes the maximum 2.5%-per-game fine on a player for missing Regular Season games, the 20% aggregate Regular Season fine cap expressly DOES apply to limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed for those same missed Regular Season games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 expressly states that 'the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension,' meaning the aggregate fine cap does NOT limit salary reductions resulting from a suspension imposed for the same missed games.
