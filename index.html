Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), which of the following MOST PRECISELY describes the scope of the exception allowing a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection to NOT count against the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts with Base Salary protection?
AA) The exception applies for the entire life of the Rookie Scale Contract, including any extended term, so long as the contract originated as a Rookie Scale Contract.
BB) The exception applies only with respect to and during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract itself, and expressly does NOT apply with respect to or during the extended term of any such Contract that has been extended.
CC) The exception applies only during the first two Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract, after which the contract counts against the seven-contract limit regardless of extension.
DD) The exception applies throughout both the original Rookie Scale Contract term and any Extension, provided the Extension does not increase Base Salary above the applicable Rookie Scale Amount.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) expressly states the exception 'shall only apply with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' Once the extended term begins, the contract counts against the seven-contract protection limit.

Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(i)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick selected 'in the Lottery,' which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rule for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is NOT protected unless waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by the Contract.
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon signing, with no triggering condition required.
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the fourth-year option.
DD) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(i), for a Lottery pick's Rookie Scale Contract, the Base Salary for the first Season 'shall be fully protected' — meaning it is fully protected immediately upon signing with no triggering condition. This distinguishes Lottery picks from other First Round Picks, whose first-Season protection is conditioned on no waivers being requested before the first day of the first Regular Season.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player and she plays four (4) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated (none under any Extension of that Contract). The Team later designates Player B and she plays two (2) Seasons under her Contract. How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) Four (4) Seasons, because only the most recent Core Player's Seasons are counted.
BB) Six (6) Seasons, because all Seasons played under a Contract signed while designated count, but Seasons under any Extension are excluded.
CC) Eight (8) Seasons, because both the original and extended terms count for all designated players.
DD) Two (2) Seasons, because only Seasons played under a Core Player Contract Extension are counted toward the limit.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) states that Seasons played under the Contract signed while a player was designated as a Core Player — but NOT including Seasons covered by any Extension — count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit. Player A's four Seasons plus Player B's two Seasons totals six (6), and no Extension Seasons are involved.
Q5Art. I (definition of 'Qualifying Offer'); Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service is eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer from her Prior Team. Per Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for such a Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) 105% of the player's fourth-year Base Salary under her prior Contract.
BB) The Standard Maximum Salary applicable to the Salary Cap Year of the offer.
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
DD) The Minimum Annual Salary for a player with four (4) or more Years of Service.
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' subsection (D) defines a 'Restricted Qualifying Offer' for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d) as 'the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.' This is distinct from a Rookie Scale Qualifying Offer, which uses 105% of the fourth-year Base Salary.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), which of the following CORRECTLY states both the condition that triggers a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for the 2028 Salary Cap Year AND the formula for computing that shortfall amount?
AA) Triggered when the Salary Cap for 2028 would have increased by more than 10% from the 2027 Salary Cap but for Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the excess above a 10% increase multiplied by the total number of Teams in the 2028 Season.
BB) Triggered when Total Salaries for 2028 are less than the 2028 Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams; the shortfall equals that difference.
CC) Triggered when the Salary Cap for 2028 would have decreased by more than 10% from the 2027 Salary Cap; the shortfall equals the excess decrease multiplied by the number of Teams.
DD) Triggered when Total Salaries fall below 85% of the Salary Cap multiplied by the number of Teams; the shortfall equals the difference between Total Salaries and 85% of the league-wide cap.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) provides that a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' arises when the Salary Cap would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for 2027) but for the cap on annual increases in Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall amount equals the additional amount above the allowed increase multiplied by the total number of Teams. Option B describes the 'Guarantee Shortfall,' not the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall.
Q7Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii), the 'Guarantee Shortfall' for a Salary Cap Year is computed when which of the following conditions is met, and how is it calculated?
AA) When a Team's Team Salary falls below 85% of the Salary Cap; computed as the difference between the Minimum Team Salary and the Team's Team Salary.
BB) When Total Salaries league-wide are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in that Season; computed as the amount of that shortfall.
CC) When Total Salaries league-wide are less than 90% of the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams; computed as the difference between 90% of aggregate caps and Total Salaries.
DD) When an individual Team's Total Salaries are less than the Minimum Team Salary; computed as the difference between those two figures multiplied by the number of Teams below the minimum.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) defines the 'Guarantee Shortfall' as arising when, at the conclusion of a Season, Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams in that Season; the shortfall equals that difference. This is a league-wide calculation, not a per-team calculation.
Q8Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states BOTH the out-of-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the out-of-pocket threshold at which out-of-network coverage reaches 100%?
AA) $1,800 annual deductible per individual; 100% coverage after the individual has paid $9,000.
BB) $2,500 annual deductible per individual; 100% coverage after the individual has paid $8,000.
CC) $6,250 annual deductible per individual; 100% coverage after the individual has paid $20,000.
DD) $2,500 annual deductible per individual; 100% coverage after the individual has paid $20,000.
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) states that for non-Toronto Tempo players, out-of-network services carry a $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual ($6,250 per family), with coverage at 60% or 70% until the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person, after which coverage reaches 100%. The $6,250 and $20,000 figures are the per-family amounts, not per-individual.
Q9Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, a player with a Base Salary of $400,000 misses seven (7) Regular Season games and four (4) playoff games in the same Season. Which of the following correctly states the MAXIMUM aggregate fines that may be imposed for the missed Regular Season games and the missed playoff games, respectively?
AA) Up to $80,000 for missed Regular Season games; up to $100,000 for missed playoff games.
BB) Up to $70,000 for missed Regular Season games; up to $80,000 for missed playoff games.
CC) Up to $80,000 for missed Regular Season games; up to $80,000 for missed playoff games.
DD) Up to $70,000 for missed Regular Season games; up to $100,000 for missed playoff games.
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 sets aggregate fine caps of 20% of Base Salary for missed Regular Season games ($400,000 × 20% = $80,000) and 25% of Base Salary for missed playoff games ($400,000 × 25% = $100,000). Per-game fine caps (2.5% per Regular Season game and 5% per playoff game) would produce lower amounts for seven and four games respectively, but the caps here are the controlling maximums.
Q10Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), the Rookie Scale Amounts for Second Round Picks and Third Round Picks in Rookie Scale Contracts commencing with Seasons after 2026 are adjusted by the same percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap that applies to First Round Picks.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) provides that First Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by the percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap, while Second Round Pick and Third Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by a flat 4% increase — not by the Salary Cap percentage change. The two mechanisms are expressly different.
