Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection is extended. At precisely which point does the exception to the seven (7) Player Contract Base Salary protection limit CEASE to apply?
AA) When the option year of the Rookie Scale Contract is exercised by the Team
BB) When the extended term of the Rookie Scale Contract begins (i.e., as of the first Season of the extended term)
CC) When the player accumulates three (3) Years of Service
DD) When the player's Base Salary under the Extension first exceeds the Minimum Player Salary
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) states that the exception to the seven (7) Contract limit for Rookie Scale Contracts 'shall only apply with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' The exception therefore ceases the moment the extended term begins.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is INELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player with exactly six (6) Years of Service signing a new Contract
BB) A player with exactly seven (7) Years of Service signing an Extension
CC) A player with exactly five (5) Years of Service signing a new Contract
DD) A player with eight (8) Years of Service signing a new Contract
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) provides that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. A player with exactly five (5) Years of Service therefore falls below the threshold and is ineligible.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv), a Contract that does NOT contain a trade bonus is extended. Which of the following CORRECTLY states both the condition under which a trade bonus may be added AND the earliest point at which that bonus first becomes payable?
AA) The bonus may be added at any time during the original term; it is payable the first time the Contract is traded after the amendment
BB) The bonus may be added simultaneously with the extension; it is payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term
CC) The bonus may be added simultaneously with the extension; it is payable upon the first trade of the Contract, regardless of timing
DD) The bonus may be added within thirty (30) days of the extension; it is payable only after the first Season of the extended term is completed
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv) states that a Contract without a trade bonus may only be amended to add one simultaneously with an extension, and the Exhibit 8 language must specify that the bonus 'shall not be applicable to any trade prior to the end of the [Season immediately preceding the extended term], but shall instead be payable only the first time this Contract is traded following the [___] Season.'
Q4Art. VI Sec. 5(b); Art. I (Qualifying Offer)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service is eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer. Per Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that offer?
AA) 105% of the player's fourth-year Base Salary under her prior Contract
BB) The Standard Maximum Salary for the applicable Salary Cap Year
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
DD) The Minimum Annual Salary for a player with 4 or more Years of Service
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' provision (D) states that for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), the Restricted Qualifying Offer must be 'the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.'
Q5Art. VII Sec. 1(c)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), the Minimum Team Salary is set at what percentage of the Salary Cap, and which of the following is a recognized EXCEPTION to the WNBA's obligation to cause a Team to make shortfall payments?
AA) 80%; a Team that finishes the Season with fewer than ten (10) players on its roster
BB) 85%; a Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season
CC) 85%; a Team whose Team Salary shortfall is less than $250,000
DD) 90%; an Expansion Team in its first Season of operations
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap. Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(ii) provides that 'any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season shall not be required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation.'
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Guarantee Shortfall' for a given Salary Cap Year is computed as the difference between Total Salaries and a specific benchmark. Which of the following CORRECTLY states that benchmark?
AA) The Salary Cap for such Salary Cap Year multiplied by the total number of Teams in the WNBA in such Season
BB) The Minimum Team Salary for such Salary Cap Year multiplied by the total number of Teams in the WNBA in such Season
CC) The Designated 20% Share of SBR for such Salary Cap Year
DD) One hundred percent (100%) of Total Benefits for such Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) defines the Guarantee Shortfall as the amount by which Total Salaries for a Salary Cap Year fall short of 'the Salary Cap for such Salary Cap Year multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in such Season,' measured as of the conclusion of the Season.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states BOTH the in-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the out-of-pocket threshold at which in-network coverage reaches 100%?
AA) $600 deductible; 100% coverage after $3,000 out-of-pocket per person
BB) $600 deductible; 100% coverage after $6,000 out-of-pocket per person
CC) $1,000 deductible; 100% coverage after $3,000 out-of-pocket per person
DD) $2,500 deductible; 100% coverage after $8,000 out-of-pocket per person
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) states that for in-network services, there is a '$600 annual deductible per covered individual ($1,800 per family), and coverage at eighty percent (80%) until the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person ($9,000 per family) in a calendar year, and one hundred percent (100%) thereafter.'

Q9Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, the per-Regular-Season-game fine cap is 2.5% of Base Salary and the aggregate fine cap for missing Regular Season games is 20% of Base Salary; these caps apply independently from any suspension salary reduction, meaning a player could lose more than 20% of Base Salary in a Season if both a Section 7 fine AND a suspension are imposed for the same missed games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 explicitly states that the 20% and 25% aggregate fine caps 'shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension,' meaning a Team retains the right to impose a suspension or other discipline for the same or other missed games, and the salary reduction from suspension is separate from the fine caps.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2031 Season and beyond, the CBA expressly provides that all non-Developmental Players, regardless of Base Salary level, are entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of their WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a) provides apartment benefits only through the 2030 Season: universally for 2026-2028, and subject to a $500,000 Base Salary cap for 2029-2030. The CBA contains no provision extending Team-provided apartments to any Season after 2030, so no such entitlement exists for the 2031 Season.
