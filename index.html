Under the 2026 CBA, what is the Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year?
AA) $5,000,000
BB) $6,000,000
CC) $7,000,000
DD) $8,000,000
CBA Reference
Article VII, Section 1(a) explicitly states that the Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year shall be $7,000,000. This is a fixed figure established at the outset of the new CBA.
Q2Art. VII §1(a)4-choice
Under Article VII, what is the maximum percentage by which the Salary Cap for the 2027 Salary Cap Year can increase or decrease from the 2026 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 10%
BB) 12%
CC) 13%
DD) 15%
CBA Reference
Article VII, Section 1(a) states that the Salary Cap for the 2027 Salary Cap Year shall not increase or decrease by more than thirteen percent (13%) from the Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year, while subsequent years are capped at 10%.
Q3Art. V §34-choice
Under the 2026 CBA, which of the following players is NOT permitted to negotiate allowable amendments to a Standard Player Contract pursuant to Article V, Section 3?
AA) A Veteran Player in her third WNBA season
BB) A Rookie selected in the first round of the WNBA Draft
CC) A Veteran Player who has been waived and re-signed
DD) A Veteran Player entering free agency
CBA Reference
Article V, Section 3 explicitly states that only a Veteran Player (but not a Rookie) and a Team may amend the provisions of a Standard Player Contract in the permissible ways listed. Rookies are expressly excluded from negotiating these amendments.
Q4Art. V §2(d)4-choice
According to Article V, Section 1(b), in what specific way may a player unilaterally change the form of the Standard Player Contract?
AA) By adding a no-trade clause to Exhibit 2
BB) By deleting paragraph 6(d) of the Standard Player Contract in its entirety
CC) By renegotiating the Base Salary structure in Exhibit 1
DD) By inserting a right of first refusal provision
CBA Reference
Article V, Section 2(d) provides that notwithstanding the general limitation on amendments, a player may unilaterally change the form of the Standard Player Contract by deleting paragraph 6(d) thereof in its entirety. This is the only unilateral amendment right granted to a player.
Q5Art. V §3(b)4-choice
Under Article V, Section 3(b), a Veteran Player may negotiate salary protection in Exhibit 2 of her Standard Player Contract for all of the following reasons EXCEPT:
AA) Lack of skill
BB) Basketball-related injury
CC) Voluntary retirement
DD) In-Season injury or illness
CBA Reference
Article V, Section 3(b) permits salary protection for (i) lack of skill, (ii) basketball-related injury, and (iii) in-Season injury or illness, among other listed reasons. Voluntary retirement is not listed as a permissible ground for salary protection in Exhibit 2.
Q6Art. VI §1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Article VI, Section 1(a), beginning on what specific date may an Unrestricted Free Agent begin to negotiate a Player Contract with any Team?
AA) January 15
BB) February 1
CC) February 4
DD) The first day following the Moratorium Period
CBA Reference
Article VI, Section 1(a)(ii) states that an Unrestricted Free Agent is free at any time beginning on February 4 to negotiate a Player Contract with any Team, though she may not enter into such a contract until the day following the last day of the Moratorium Period.
Q7Art. VI §2(b)T/F
True or False: Under Article VI, Section 2(b), a player may be subject to a Right of First Refusal as a result of playing in a professional basketball league other than the WNBA.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Article VI, Section 2(b) explicitly states that no Right of First Refusal rule, practice, policy, regulation, or agreement providing for a Right of First Refusal shall be applied to any player as a result of that player's entry into a player contract with or playing with any team in any professional basketball league other than the WNBA.
Q8Art. X §1(a)4-choice
Under Article X, if a player's Standard Player Contract is terminated AFTER the Mid-Point of the Regular Season, how long does she continue to receive medical benefits?
AA) 30 days following contract termination
BB) 60 days following contract termination
CC) Until the end of the calendar year
DD) Until the end of the WNBA Season
CBA Reference
Article X, Section 1(a) specifies that a player whose Standard Player Contract is terminated after the Mid-Point of the Regular Season will continue to receive medical benefits until the end of the calendar year, at the conclusion of which she will be provided with a notice of COBRA rights.
Q9Art. XIV §1T/F
True or False: Under Article XIV, Section 1, a player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another Team is explicitly identified as being in violation of the player conduct standards.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Article XIV, Section 1 explicitly states, 'For avoidance of doubt, any player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another Team shall be in violation of this Section 1.' This is a notable and specific provision in the conduct standards.
