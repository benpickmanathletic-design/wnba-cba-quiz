Q1Art. VII §1(a)4-choice
Under the 2026 CBA, what is the exact Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year?
AA) $6,000,000
BB) $6,500,000
CC) $7,000,000
DD) $7,500,000
CBA Reference
Article VII, Section 1(a) explicitly states: 'The Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year shall be $7,000,000.' This is a fixed figure set in the CBA, not derived from the SBR formula.
Q2Art. VII §1(a)4-choice
Under Art. VII, what is the maximum percentage by which the Salary Cap can increase or decrease from 2026 to 2027, and how does that differ from subsequent years?
AA) 10% for 2027, 10% for all subsequent years
BB) 13% for 2027, 10% for all subsequent years
CC) 15% for 2027, 13% for all subsequent years
DD) 13% for 2027, 13% for all subsequent years
CBA Reference
Art. VII Section 1(a) states the Salary Cap for 2027 shall not increase or decrease by more than 13% from 2026, while each Salary Cap Year following 2027 shall not increase or decrease by more than 10% from the immediately preceding year. This creates a special transition rule for 2027.
Q3Art. VII §1(b)4-choice
Under Art. VII, if the Salary Cap for a given year would have increased by 16% but is capped at 10%, the 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' is calculated as:
AA) The full 16% increase multiplied by number of Teams
BB) The 6% excess above the 10% cap multiplied by number of Teams
CC) The 6% excess above the 10% cap divided by number of Teams
DD) The difference between 16% and 13% multiplied by number of Teams
CBA Reference
Art. VII Section 1(b) defines the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall as the additional amount by which the Salary Cap would have increased but for the cap operation — i.e., the amount in excess of the permitted 10% (or 13%) increase — multiplied by the total number of Teams. The excess percentage (6%) times the per-team cap times total teams yields the shortfall.
Q4Art. V §3(a)4-choice
Under Art. V, which of the following allowable amendments to a Standard Player Contract applies ONLY to Veteran Players and NOT to Rookies?
AA) Setting the term length of the contract
BB) Negotiating Base Salary amounts set forth in Exhibit 1
CC) Waiving the mandatory arbitration clause
DD) Adding a no-trade clause approved by the Commissioner
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 3 explicitly states that 'a Veteran Player (but not a Rookie) and a Team may amend the provisions of a Standard Player Contract,' with allowable amendments including agreeing upon Base Salary provisions set forth in Exhibit 1. Rookies are bound by the Rookie Scale under Art. VIII and cannot individually negotiate these terms.
Q5Art. V §3(b)4-choice
Under Art. V, Section 3(b), which of the following types of salary protection is NOT listed as an allowable basis for protecting a player's Base Salary in Exhibit 2 of a Standard Player Contract?
AA) Lack of skill
BB) Basketball-related injury
CC) In-Season injury or illness
DD) Voluntary retirement by the player
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 3(b) lists the allowable bases for salary protection as: (i) lack of skill, (ii) basketball-related injury, (iii) in-Season injury or illness, and (iv) a fourth category (cut off in the text). Voluntary retirement is not listed as an allowable protection basis and would not be a permitted amendment.
Q6Art. V §2(d)4-choice
Under Art. V, a player may unilaterally modify the Standard Player Contract form by:
AA) Adding a marketing rights carve-out in Exhibit 2
BB) Deleting paragraph 6(d) of the Standard Player Contract in its entirety
CC) Shortening the contract term to one season
DD) Removing the arbitration provision with written notice to the WNBA
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 2(d) states: 'Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Section 2, a player may unilaterally change the form of the Standard Player Contract by deleting paragraph 6(d) thereof in its entirety.' This is the sole unilateral modification right granted to players.
Q7Art. VI §1(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, which category of free agent is subject to a Right of First Refusal?
AA) Unrestricted Free Agent
BB) Reserved Player
CC) Restricted Free Agent
DD) Core Player
CBA Reference
Art. VI Section 1(b) states: 'No right of first refusal shall be applicable to any Free Agent other than a Restricted Free Agent.' All other free agent categories — including Unrestricted, Reserved, and Core Players — are explicitly exempt from any Right of First Refusal obligation.
Q8Art. VI §1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VI, beginning on what date may an Unrestricted Free Agent begin to negotiate a Player Contract with ANY Team?
AA) January 1
BB) February 4
CC) The first day of the Moratorium Period
DD) The day following the last day of the Moratorium Period
CBA Reference
Art. VI Section 1(a)(ii) specifies that an Unrestricted Free Agent 'is free at any time beginning on February 4 to negotiate' a Player Contract with any Team. However, she may not enter into (i.e., sign) such a contract until the day following the last day of the Moratorium Period.
Q9Art. XIV §1T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, a player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another Team is explicitly identified as being in violation of the Player Conduct standards.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV Section 1 states explicitly: 'For avoidance of doubt, any player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another Team shall be in violation of this Section 1.' This is a notable and specific provision of the 2026 CBA.
Q10Art. X §1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. X, a player whose Standard Player Contract is terminated AFTER the Mid-Point of the Regular Season will continue to receive medical benefits for only 30 days following termination.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. X Section 1(a) distinguishes between two scenarios: a player terminated BEFORE the Mid-Point receives 30 days of continued coverage, while a player terminated AFTER the Mid-Point continues to receive medical benefits until the END of the calendar year — a significantly longer protection period.
