Q1Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2030 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
CC) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) expressly states that for the 2030 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year. This distinguishes 2030 from 2029 (which uses 12.25%) and from 2031/2032 (which use 13%).
Q2Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' arises when which of the following conditions is met?
AA) Total Salaries for a Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams
BB) The Salary Cap for a Salary Cap Year would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for 2027) but for the cap-smoothing rule in Sec. 1(a)(ii)
CC) A Team's Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary at the conclusion of a Season
DD) The Salary Cap for a Salary Cap Year decreases by more than 10% from the prior year
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) defines the 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' as arising when the Salary Cap would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for 2027) from the prior year but for the operation of the cap-smoothing ceiling in Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the additional increase that was suppressed multiplied by the number of Teams. This is distinct from the 'Guarantee Shortfall,' which addresses Total Salaries falling below aggregate cap.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, which of the following correctly describes the Base Salary protection status for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon signing
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the first Regular Season
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season may contain conditional protection only
DD) Such a Rookie Scale Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii) explicitly states: 'For clarity, such a Rookie Scale Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season of such Contract.' Protection for Second and Third Round Picks begins only with the second Season, conditioned on waivers not being requested prior to the start of that Season.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), which of the following correctly states the maximum number of Core Player designations a Team may make AND the treatment of Seasons played under a Core Player Contract Extension toward that maximum?
AA) A Team may designate no more than two (2) Core Players over the life of the CBA; Seasons under an Extension of a Core Player Contract count toward the maximum
BB) A Team may designate no more than one (1) Core Player at a time; Seasons covered by any Extension to a Core Player Contract are NOT included in the maximum count
CC) A Team may designate no more than one (1) Core Player at a time; all Seasons played under the original and extended term count toward the maximum
DD) A Team may designate no more than two (2) Core Players; Seasons under Extensions do not count toward the maximum
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that each Team may designate no more than one (1) Veteran Free Agent as a Core Player, and that Seasons played while so designated (but NOT including Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract) count toward the maximum. This distinction between original Contract Seasons and Extension Seasons is a precise and commonly-tested nuance.
Q5Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(v)4-choice
Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a player whose Base Salary is exactly $500,001 is NOT entitled to a Team-provided apartment. Under the same section, what housing benefit, if any, does the CBA provide for a player in this salary range whose Contract is assigned from one Team to another mid-Season?
AA) The assignee Team must provide a one-bedroom apartment for the remainder of the Season
BB) The assignee Team must provide an individual hotel room in the assignee Team's home city for up to thirty-five (35) days following the assignment
CC) The assignee Team must reimburse the player up to $5,000 for lease payments in the new home city
DD) No housing benefit is owed; the player is solely responsible for her own housing
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(v) states that for any player not entitled to Team-provided housing and whose Contract is assigned from one Team to another during the Season, the assignee Team will provide an individual hotel room in the assignee Team's home city for up to thirty-five (35) days following the assignment. The $5,000 lease reimbursement applies only to players whose Contracts are terminated, not assigned.
Q6Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), a player has exactly five (5) Years of Service and signs a new Contract (not an Extension). Which of the following correctly describes the trade bonus eligibility for that Contract?
AA) The Contract may contain a trade bonus because the player has at least five (5) Years of Service
BB) The Contract may not contain a trade bonus because the player has fewer than six (6) Years of Service
CC) The Contract may contain a trade bonus only if the player waives her right to base salary protection
DD) The Contract may contain a trade bonus only if it is simultaneously executed with an Extension
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) expressly provides that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. A player with exactly five (5) Years of Service has not yet reached the six (6) Year threshold and is therefore ineligible.
Q7Art. VII Sec. 1(c)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), the Minimum Team Salary is set at what percentage of the Salary Cap, and which of the following correctly states an exception to the WNBA's obligation to cause a Team to make shortfall payments?
AA) 80%; Teams in their first expansion Season are exempt
BB) 85%; any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season is not required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation
CC) 85%; any Team that has fewer than twelve (12) players on its roster at season's end is exempt
DD) 80%; any Team that has used all available Salary Cap Exceptions is exempt
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap for the applicable Salary Cap Year. Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(ii) then provides the sole express exception: any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season shall not be required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
Q8Art. VIII Sec. (b) and (c)4-choice
Under Art. VIII, Sec. (b) and (c), what is the Rookie Scale Amount for the overall SECOND pick in the 2026 WNBA Draft for the FIRST Season of her Rookie Scale Contract, and for Rookie Scale Contracts commencing after 2026, how are Second Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts adjusted?
AA) $466,913 for Season 1; adjusted by the same percentage change in the Salary Cap as First Round Picks
BB) $485,590 for Season 1; increased by a fixed 4% annually
CC) $466,913 for Season 1; increased by a fixed 4% annually
DD) $500,000 for Season 1; adjusted by the percentage change in the Salary Cap
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (b) sets the first-Season Rookie Scale Amount for the second overall pick at $466,913 for contracts commencing with the 2026 Season. Art. VIII, Sec. (c) then provides that for contracts commencing after 2026, Second Round (and Third Round) Pick amounts are increased by a fixed 4%, whereas First Round Pick amounts are adjusted by the percentage change in the Salary Cap.
Q9Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv)T/F
True or False: Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), a Contract that does not contain a trade bonus may be amended after execution to add a trade bonus at any point during its term, provided only that the player has at least six (6) Years of Service at the time of the amendment.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
False. Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv) provides that a Contract that does not contain a trade bonus cannot be amended to add one, with a single exception: if the Contract is extended, a trade bonus may be added simultaneously with the Extension. Merely having six (6) or more Years of Service does not allow a mid-term amendment to add a trade bonus absent an Extension.
Q10Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)(E)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' formula for the 2031 and 2032 Salary Cap Years both use 30.5% of League Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
True. Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii)(E) expressly states that for each of the 2031 and 2032 Salary Cap Years, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year, making the formula identical for both years.
