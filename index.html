Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(ii) and (vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii), a trade bonus in a Contract may not exceed what percentage of the Base Salary remaining to be earned by the player at the time of the trade, and in no event may that bonus be payable more than how many times across the life of the Contract?
AA) 15% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than twice
BB) 10% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than once
CC) 10% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than twice
DD) 5% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than once
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii) caps a trade bonus at ten percent (10%) of the Base Salary remaining to be earned at the time of the trade. Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) further provides that in no event shall a trade bonus be payable more than once.
Q2Art. V Sec. 4(e)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick who was NOT selected 'in the Lottery,' which of the following CORRECTLY describes the Base Salary protection status for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon signing.
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by the Contract.
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season is never protected; protection only applies beginning in Season 2.
DD) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected only if the Team exercises the option for the fourth Season.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(ii), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by any First Round Pick other than a Lottery pick, the Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract. This is distinct from a Lottery pick, whose first Season is immediately fully protected upon signing.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), which of the following CORRECTLY states the exception to the seven (7) Player Contract Base Salary protection limit for a Rookie Scale Contract that contains Base Salary protection?
AA) The exception applies only during Seasons 1 and 2 of the Rookie Scale Contract and ceases upon the Team's exercise of the fourth-year option.
BB) The exception applies during all four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract and ceases to apply upon the conclusion of the extended term of any Extension of that Contract.
CC) The exception applies with respect to and during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract and ceases to apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Extension of that Rookie Scale Contract.
DD) The exception applies indefinitely, including during any Extension, as long as the player was originally signed as a Draft Rookie.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) expressly states that the exception for Rookie Scale Contracts 'shall only apply with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' Once the Contract is extended, the exception ceases and the protected Rookie Scale Contract counts against the seven-Contract limit.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays two (2) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated but NOT including any extension of that Contract. The Team subsequently designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays three (3) Seasons under her Contract. How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) 3 Seasons (only Player B's Seasons count because Player A's designation ended first)
BB) 5 Seasons total (2 from Player A + 3 from Player B)
CC) 2 Seasons (only Player A's Seasons count because she was designated first)
DD) 6 Seasons because extensions are included for both players
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that Seasons played under a Contract signed while a player was designated as a Core Player (but NOT including Seasons covered by any Extension of that Contract) count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit. Here, 2 Seasons from Player A and 3 Seasons from Player B total 5 Seasons counted against the limit.
Q5Art. I (Qualifying Offer definition (D)); Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service has her Prior Team make a Restricted Qualifying Offer. Per Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) 105% of the player's fourth-year Base Salary from her prior Rookie Scale Contract
BB) The applicable Supermax Salary for the upcoming Season
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
DD) The Standard Maximum Salary for a player with four (4) Years of Service
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' subsection (D) states that for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), the Restricted Qualifying Offer must be at least 'the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.' This floor ensures the player is not offered less than she previously earned.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), the 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for the 2027 Salary Cap Year is triggered when which specific condition is met, and how is the shortfall amount computed?
AA) When the Salary Cap for 2027 would have increased by more than 10% but for the cap; the shortfall equals the excess above 10% multiplied by total Teams.
BB) When the Salary Cap for 2027 would have increased by more than 13% but for the cap; the shortfall equals the excess above 13% multiplied by total Teams.
CC) When Total Salaries for 2027 fall below the Salary Cap multiplied by total Teams; the shortfall equals that difference.
DD) When the Salary Cap for 2027 would have decreased by more than 13%; the shortfall equals the excess decrease multiplied by total Teams.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) provides that a Salary Cap Limit Shortfall arises for the 2027 Salary Cap Year specifically when the Salary Cap would have increased by more than thirteen percent (13%) from the 2026 Salary Cap but for the operation of the 13% cap. The shortfall equals the amount in excess of the 13% increase multiplied by the total number of Teams in the WNBA that Season.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states the out-of-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the point at which out-of-network coverage reaches 100%?
AA) $1,500 deductible per individual; 100% after $6,000 out-of-pocket per person per calendar year
BB) $2,500 deductible per individual; 100% after $8,000 out-of-pocket per person per calendar year
CC) $600 deductible per individual; 100% after $3,000 out-of-pocket per person per calendar year
DD) $2,500 deductible per individual; 100% after $6,250 out-of-pocket per person per calendar year
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) specifies that for out-of-network services for non-Toronto Tempo players, the annual deductible is $2,500 per covered individual (with $6,250 per family), and coverage reaches 100% after the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person ($20,000 per family) in a calendar year. The $6,250 figure is the family deductible, not the individual out-of-pocket threshold.

Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2029 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
BB) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) explicitly provides that for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year. The Team Revenue percentage steps up from 12% (2028) to 12.25% (2029) to 12.5% (2030).
Q9Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), the Rookie Scale Amounts for Second Round Picks and Third Round Picks in Rookie Scale Contracts commencing with Seasons after 2026 are adjusted by the percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap — the same mechanism used for First Round Picks.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) expressly distinguishes the two adjustment mechanisms: First Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by the percentage increase or decrease in the Salary Cap, while Second Round Pick and Third Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by a flat 4% increase each Season, regardless of Salary Cap movement.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2031 Season and beyond, the CBA expressly provides that all non-Developmental Players, regardless of Base Salary level, are entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of their WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a) only expressly addresses housing for the 2026–2028 Seasons (all non-Developmental Players) and the 2029–2030 Seasons (only those with Base Salary at or below $500,000). The CBA makes no express provision for Team-provided housing for the 2031 Season or beyond, so the statement is false.
