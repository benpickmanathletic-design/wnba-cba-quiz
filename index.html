Q1Art. VII s1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Article VII, Section 1, what is the maximum percentage by which the Salary Cap for the 2027 Salary Cap Year can increase or decrease from the 2026 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 10%
BB) 12%
CC) 13%
DD) 15%
CBA Reference
Art. VII Section 1(a)(ii) explicitly states the 2027 Salary Cap shall not increase or decrease by more than thirteen percent (13%) from the 2026 Salary Cap. This is a special transitional rule; all subsequent years are capped at 10%.
Q2Art. VII s1(a)(ii)4-choice
The 2026 WNBA Salary Cap is set at $7,000,000. If the formula-derived cap for 2027 would have been $8,400,000 (a 20% increase), what is the maximum allowable 2027 Salary Cap?
AA) $7,700,000
BB) $7,910,000
CC) $8,050,000
DD) $8,400,000
CBA Reference
Art. VII Section 1(a)(ii) caps the 2027 increase at 13% over 2026. 13% of $7,000,000 = $910,000, so the maximum 2027 cap is $7,910,000. The excess $490,000 per team would factor into the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall calculation.
Q3Art. VII s1(b)4-choice
Under Article VII, Section 1(b), the WNBA is obligated to make Guarantee Payments to players when which of the following conditions is met?
AA) Total Salaries exceed the aggregate Salary Cap across all teams
BB) The formula-derived cap increase exceeded 10% (or 13% for 2027) but was limited by the cap ceiling rule, AND Total Salaries fall below aggregate Salary Cap
CC) Any individual team's payroll falls below its Salary Cap floor
DD) The Salary Cap decreases from one year to the next
CBA Reference
Art. VII Section 1(b) creates two components of WNBA payment obligation: a Salary Cap Limit Shortfall (when the growth cap suppresses the cap) and a Guarantee Shortfall (when Total Salaries across all teams fall below aggregate Salary Cap). Both conditions are relevant to the guarantee payment obligation.
Q4Art. V s34-choice
Under Article V, Section 3, which category of player is expressly PROHIBITED from negotiating allowable amendments to a Standard Player Contract?
AA) Veteran Players in their first contract year
BB) Rookies
CC) Core Players
DD) Restricted Free Agents
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 3 explicitly states 'a Veteran Player (but not a Rookie) and a Team may amend the provisions of a Standard Player Contract.' Rookies are categorically excluded from negotiating allowable amendments such as salary protection provisions.
Q5Art. V s3(b)4-choice
Article V, Section 3(b) permits salary protection in a Standard Player Contract for which of the following circumstances?
AA) Termination due to lack of skill only
BB) Termination due to basketball-related injury and in-Season injury or illness, but not lack of skill
CC) Termination due to lack of skill, basketball-related injury, and in-Season injury or illness
DD) Termination for any reason at the Team's discretion
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 3(b) permits salary protection in Exhibit 2 to the Standard Player Contract for: (i) lack of skill; (ii) basketball-related injury; and (iii) disability from any injury or illness during a WNBA Season. All three are explicitly listed as allowable protections.
Q6Art. V s2(d)4-choice
Under Article V, Section 2(d), a player may unilaterally modify a Standard Player Contract by deleting which specific paragraph?
AA) Paragraph 6(b)
BB) Paragraph 6(c)
CC) Paragraph 6(d)
DD) Paragraph 7(a)
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 2(d) provides a narrow unilateral right: a player may delete paragraph 6(d) of the Standard Player Contract in its entirety. This is the only unilateral amendment right preserved for players under Section 2.
Q7Art. VI s1(a)(iv)4-choice
Under Article VI, Section 1, a Core Player is free to begin negotiating a new Player Contract with her Prior Team beginning on what date?
AA) January 15
BB) February 4
CC) The day after the Moratorium Period ends
DD) March 1
CBA Reference
Art. VI Section 1(a)(iv) specifies that a Core Player may begin negotiating with her Prior Team on February 4, but cannot actually enter into the contract until the day following the last day of the Moratorium Period. Negotiation rights and signing rights have different trigger dates.
Q8Art. VI s1(b)4-choice
Under Article VI, Section 1(b), which type of free agent is subject to a Right of First Refusal?
AA) Unrestricted Free Agent
BB) Core Player
CC) Restricted Free Agent
DD) Any Free Agent who withholds services
CBA Reference
Art. VI Section 1(b) states that no Right of First Refusal applies to any Free Agent 'other than a Restricted Free Agent.' All other free agent classifications—Unrestricted, Core—have no ROFR applicable and no compensation obligation to any team.
Q9Art. XIV s1T/F
True or False: Under Article XIV, Section 1, a player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded is explicitly identified as being in violation of her player conduct obligations.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV Section 1 explicitly states 'For avoidance of doubt, any player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another Team shall be in violation of this Section 1.' This is a notable and specific provision added to the 2026 CBA.
Q10Art. VIIIT/F
True or False: Under Article VIII, the 2026 Rookie Scale sets the first-year Base Salary for the first overall pick at $500,000.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII expressly lists Pick 1 first-year Base Salary as $500,000, with subsequent years at $520,000, $572,000, and a fourth-year option at $646,360. This represents a significant increase from prior CBA rookie salaries and applies to Rookie Scale Contracts commencing with the 2026 Season.
