Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection does NOT count against a Team's per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts with Base Salary protection. During which precise window does this exception apply, and when does it cease?
AA) The exception applies for all four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract and continues through any extended term of that Contract
BB) The exception applies only with respect to and during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract, and ceases to apply with respect to or during the extended term of any such Contract that has been extended
CC) The exception applies only during the first two Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract, and ceases upon exercise of the Team's Fourth Year Option
DD) The exception applies only during the initial three-Season term of the Rookie Scale Contract, and ceases upon exercise of the Fourth Year Option regardless of any Extension
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) expressly states the exception 'shall only apply with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' Once a Rookie Scale Contract is extended, the exception no longer applies.
Q2Art. V Sec. 4(e)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick who was NOT selected 'in the Lottery,' which of the following CORRECTLY describes the Base Salary protection status for the FIRST Season of such Contract under the 2026 CBA?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected from the date of signing with no conditions
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season shall become fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract
CC) The first Season Base Salary may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind
DD) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the Fourth Year Option
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(ii) specifies that for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by any First Round Pick not selected in the Lottery, the Base Salary for the first Season shall become fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract. This is a conditional protection tied to the waiver deadline.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv), a Player Contract that does NOT already contain a trade bonus may be amended to add one only under what specific circumstance, and what restriction applies to when that added bonus is payable?
AA) A trade bonus may be added by amendment at any time before the trade occurs, and is payable the first time the Contract is traded after the amendment
BB) A trade bonus may be added only if the Contract is extended, and shall be payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term of the Extension
CC) A trade bonus may be added only with WNBA Commissioner approval and is payable the second time the Contract is traded after the amendment
DD) A trade bonus may never be added to a Contract that did not originally contain one
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv) provides that a Contract without a trade bonus may be amended to add one only if the Contract is extended, and the bonus shall be payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term. The Extension's Exhibit 8 must include specific language identifying that Season.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays two (2) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated, then one (1) Season under an Extension of that Contract. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays three (3) Seasons under her Contract (no extension). How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) Six (6) Seasons total — all Seasons for both players count
BB) Five (5) Seasons total — two for Player A (Seasons under the original Contract only, not the extended term) plus three for Player B
CC) Four (4) Seasons total — one Season for Player A plus three for Player B
DD) Three (3) Seasons total — only Player B's Seasons count because Player A's designation was superseded
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) states that only Seasons played under the Contract signed while the player was designated — 'but not including Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract' — count toward the Team's Core Player limit. Player A contributes two Seasons (original Contract only, not the one Extension Season), and Player B contributes three, for a total of five.

AA) 105% of the player's Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract

Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for the 2028 Salary Cap Year arises when which specific condition is met, and how is the shortfall amount computed?
AA) When the Salary Cap for 2028 would have decreased but for Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the decrease amount multiplied by the number of Teams
BB) When the Salary Cap for 2028 would have increased by more than 10% over the 2027 Salary Cap but for Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the excess amount above a 10% increase multiplied by the total number of Teams in the 2028 Season
CC) When Total Salaries for 2028 fall below the Salary Cap multiplied by the number of Teams; the shortfall equals that difference
DD) When the Salary Cap for 2028 would have increased by more than 13% over the 2027 Salary Cap but for Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the excess above 13% multiplied by the number of Teams
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) provides that a Salary Cap Limit Shortfall arises when the Salary Cap would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for 2027 only) but for the cap on annual increases in Sec. 1(a)(ii). For the 2028 Salary Cap Year, the applicable threshold is 10% (not 13%), and the shortfall equals the amount exceeding that 10% increase multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in the 2028 Season.

Q8Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii); Art. I Sec. (aaa)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2033 Salary Cap Year, given what the CBA expressly provides?
AA) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, because the CBA's '2031 and 2032' formula language extends by implication to 2033
BB) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, reverting to the 2027 formula
CC) 31% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year plus 13.5% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, as an extrapolated increase
DD) The CBA does not provide a formula for 2033 because the Agreement's final Salary Cap Year defined in Art. I is 2032, making 2033 outside the scope of the Agreement
CBA Reference
Art. I, Sec. (aaa) defines the Salary Cap Years under this Agreement and lists only through the 2032 Salary Cap Year. Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) provides the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' formula only through 2032 (using the '2031 and 2032' provision). There is no 2033 Salary Cap Year defined in the CBA, so no formula is provided for it.
Q9Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, the 20% and 25% aggregate fine caps (for Regular Season and playoff games, respectively) DO apply to limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed for the same missed games.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 expressly states that 'the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension.' The Section 7 fine caps do not limit salary reductions resulting from a separately imposed suspension for the same missed games.
Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2031 Season and beyond, the CBA expressly entitles all non-Developmental Players, regardless of Base Salary level, to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of their WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a) provides Team-provided housing for all non-Developmental Players in 2026–2028, and for those earning $500,000 or less in 2029–2030. The CBA contains no provision extending housing entitlements to 2031 or beyond for any player, making this statement false.
