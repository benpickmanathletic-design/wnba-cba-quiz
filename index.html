Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv) and (vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv), a Contract that does NOT contain a trade bonus is extended. Which of the following CORRECTLY states BOTH the condition under which a trade bonus may be added AND the specific language required in Exhibit 8 to that Extension?
AA trade bonus may be added only if the player has at least six (6) Years of Service at the time of extension; Exhibit 8 must state that the bonus is not applicable to any trade prior to the end of the Season immediately preceding the extended term.
BA trade bonus may be added only if the player has at least six (6) Years of Service at the time of extension; Exhibit 8 need not include any special limiting language beyond specifying the bonus amount.
CA trade bonus may be added regardless of Years of Service; Exhibit 8 must state that the bonus is payable only the second time the Contract is traded following the extension.
DA trade bonus may be added only if the player has at least four (4) Years of Service at the time of extension; Exhibit 8 must state that the bonus is not applicable to any trade prior to the first Season of the extended term.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv), a Contract without a trade bonus may be amended to add one simultaneously with an Extension, but only if the player has at least six (6) Years of Service (per Sec. 3(g)(vi)). Exhibit 8 must include the specific sentence stating that the bonus 'shall not be applicable to any trade prior to the end of the [Season immediately preceding the extended term].'
Q2Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Guarantee Shortfall' for the 2028 Salary Cap Year arises when which of the following conditions is met at the conclusion of the 2028 Season?
ATotal Salaries for the 2028 Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap for the 2028 Salary Cap Year multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in the 2028 Season.
BThe Salary Cap for the 2028 Salary Cap Year would have increased by more than 10% from the 2027 Salary Cap but for the operation of Section 1(a)(ii).
CTotal Salaries for the 2028 Salary Cap Year are less than 85% of the Salary Cap for the 2028 Salary Cap Year multiplied by the total number of Teams.
DAny single Team's Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary for the 2028 Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii), the Guarantee Shortfall equals the amount by which (A) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year is less than (B) the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams participating that Season. Answer C describes the Minimum Team Salary threshold and D describes a single-team scenario; B describes the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall, not the Guarantee Shortfall.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice

AThe exception applies for the entire initial and option term (four Seasons) of the Rookie Scale Contract but does NOT apply during the extended term of that Contract.
BThe exception applies throughout the entire term of the Rookie Scale Contract, including any extended term.
CThe exception applies only during the first two Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract's initial term and ceases upon the Team's exercise of the fourth-year option.

CBA Reference
Art. AA Veteran Free Agent with exactly three (3) Years of Service; the floor is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract.
BA Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service; the floor is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract.
CA Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service; the floor is 105% of her fourth-year Base Salary.
DA Veteran Free Agent with exactly three (3) Years of Service; the floor is 105% of her prior Contract's last Season Base Salary.
CBA Reference

I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' (subsection D) states that for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Sec. 5(b)-(d), the Restricted Qualifying Offer floor is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.

A30.5% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year.
B30.5% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year.
C30.5% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year.
D30.25% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Art. For 2032, the immediately preceding year is 2031.

AOut-of-network annual deductible: $2,500 per individual; 100% coverage threshold: $8,000 per person.
BOut-of-network annual deductible: $1,800 per individual; 100% coverage threshold: $9,000 per person.
COut-of-network annual deductible: $2,500 per individual; 100% coverage threshold: $6,250 per person.
DOut-of-network annual deductible: $6,250 per individual; 100% coverage threshold: $20,000 per person.

Q7Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, a player with a Base Salary of $300,000 misses eight (8) Regular Season games and three (3) playoff games in the same Season. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the maximum aggregate fine the Team may impose for the missed Regular Season games AND the maximum aggregate fine for the missed playoff games?
AMaximum for missed Regular Season games: $60,000 (20% of $300,000); maximum for missed playoff games: $75,000 (25% of $300,000).
BMaximum for missed Regular Season games: $60,000 (20% of $300,000); maximum for missed playoff games: $45,000 (15% of $300,000).
CMaximum for missed Regular Season games: $75,000 (25% of $300,000); maximum for missed playoff games: $60,000 (20% of $300,000).
DMaximum for missed Regular Season games: $20,000 (per-game rate of 2.5% × 8 games × $300,000 without any cap); maximum for missed playoff games: $45,000 (per-game rate of 5% × 3 games × $300,000).
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 caps aggregate fines at (i) 20% of Base Salary for missing Regular Season games and (ii) 25% of Base Salary for missing playoff games in any Season. For a $300,000 Base Salary, those caps are $60,000 and $75,000 respectively, and the two caps operate independently.

Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii) and (v)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a player who is NOT a Developmental Player and whose Base Salary is exactly $500,000 is entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment, but a player with a Base Salary of $500,001 is NOT entitled to Team-provided housing and instead receives up to 35 days of individual hotel room coverage from an assignee Team only if her Contract is assigned mid-Season.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) provides Team housing for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons only to non-Developmental players 'whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000,' so a player at exactly $500,000 qualifies but one at $500,001 does not. Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(v) then provides that any player not entitled to Team-provided housing whose Contract is assigned receives up to 35 days of individual hotel room coverage from the assignee Team.
Q10Art. VII Sec. 1(a)(ii) and Sec. 1(b)(i)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), the 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for the 2027 Salary Cap Year is calculated using a 13% cap on Salary Cap increases (rather than the 10% cap used for all other post-2027 Salary Cap Years), and the resulting shortfall amount is multiplied by the total number of Teams in the WNBA that Season before being distributed to players.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) confirms that for the 2027 Salary Cap Year the applicable cap on increases is 13% (versus 10% for subsequent years), and the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall equals the excess amount above that 13% threshold multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA that Season, consistent with the general formula in Sec. 1(b)(i).
