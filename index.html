Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), which specific category of suspended player is expressly carved out of the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection of any kind, such that her Contract does NOT count against that limit?
AA) A player suspended by the WNBA for a minimum of five (5) games for a violent felony conviction under Art. XIV, Sec. 10
BB) A player suspended for the entire final Season of her Player Contract pursuant to Art. XIV, Sec. 6 or Art. XX, Sec. 1(c)
CC) A player suspended without pay under Art. XIV, Sec. 9 for failing to report by the start of the Season
DD) A player suspended by her Team for a holdout under Art. XIV, Sec. 6 for any portion of the Season
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(ii) expressly provides that the Player Contract of a player who has been suspended for the entire final Season of such Player Contract pursuant to Art. XIV, Sec. 6 or Art. XX, Sec. 1(c) shall not count against the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection of any kind. No other suspension category receives this carve-out.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is ELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player with exactly five (5) Years of Service at the time she signs the Contract or Extension
BB) A player with exactly four (4) Years of Service at the time she signs the Contract or Extension
CC) A player with exactly six (6) Years of Service at the time she signs the Contract or Extension
DD) A player with exactly three (3) Years of Service at the time she signs the Contract or Extension
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) expressly provides that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. Therefore, only a player with six (6) or more Years of Service at the time of signing is eligible; a player with exactly six (6) Years of Service meets this threshold.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(i)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick selected 'in the Lottery,' which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rule for the SECOND Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the second Season is fully protected from the date the Contract is signed
BB) The Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by such Contract
CC) The Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the fourth-year option
DD) The Base Salary for the second Season is not protected under any circumstances for a Lottery pick
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(i), for a Lottery pick's Rookie Scale Contract, the first Season is fully protected immediately, but the Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected only if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by the Contract. The fourth-year option triggers protection only for the third and fourth Seasons.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays four (4) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated, then one (1) Season under an Extension of that Contract. The Team later designates Player B and she plays three (3) Seasons under her Contract (no extension). How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) Eight (8) Seasons total
BB) Seven (7) Seasons total
CC) Nine (9) Seasons total
DD) Five (5) Seasons total
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that only Seasons played under the Contract signed while the player was designated (but NOT Seasons under any Extension) count toward the maximum Core Player limit. Player A contributes four (4) Seasons (the Extension Season does not count); Player B contributes three (3) Seasons, for a total of seven (7) Seasons.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b); Art. I (Qualifying Offer)(D)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) and Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Veteran Free Agent with exactly five (5) Years of Service receives a Restricted Qualifying Offer from her Prior Team. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) 105% of the player's fourth-year Base Salary under her prior Rookie Scale Contract
BB) The applicable Supermax Salary for the upcoming Season
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
DD) The Standard Maximum Salary for the upcoming Season
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' subsection (D) establishes that for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), the Restricted Qualifying Offer floor is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract. A player with five (5) Years of Service falls under Sec. 5(b).
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for the 2028 Salary Cap Year arises only when what specific condition is met, and how is the shortfall amount computed?
AA) When the Salary Cap for 2028 would have decreased by more than 10% from 2027 but for Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the suppressed decrease multiplied by the number of Teams
BB) When the Salary Cap for 2028 would have increased by more than 10% over the 2027 Salary Cap but for Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the excess increase amount (above the 10% cap) multiplied by the total number of Teams in the 2028 Season
CC) When Total Salaries for 2028 are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the number of Teams; the shortfall equals that deficit
DD) When the Salary Cap for 2028 would have increased by more than 13% over the 2027 Salary Cap but for Art. VII, Sec. 1(a)(ii); the shortfall equals the excess above 13% multiplied by the number of Teams
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) provides that the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall arises when the Salary Cap for a Salary Cap Year would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for the 2027 Cap Year) but for the cap on Salary Cap growth in Sec. 1(a)(ii); the 2028 Cap Year uses the 10% threshold (not 13%), and the shortfall equals the additional amount the Cap would have increased above that 10% threshold multiplied by the total number of Teams.
Q7Art. VII Sec. 1(c)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), which of the following CORRECTLY states BOTH the Minimum Team Salary as a percentage of the Salary Cap AND the ONE recognized exception to the WNBA's obligation to cause a Team to make Minimum Team Salary shortfall payments?
AA) 80% of the Salary Cap; exception: any Team that finishes below .500 in its most recent Season
BB) 85% of the Salary Cap; exception: any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season
CC) 90% of the Salary Cap; exception: any Team that is an Expansion Team in its first Season
DD) 85% of the Salary Cap; exception: any Team that has fewer than twelve (12) players on its roster as of the conclusion of the Season
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at 85% of the Salary Cap, and Sec. 1(c)(ii) expressly provides that any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season shall not be required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation—this is the sole recognized exception stated in the CBA.
Q8Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states BOTH the out-of-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the percentage of most eligible charges covered BEFORE the out-of-pocket threshold is reached?
AA) $2,500 deductible; 70% of most eligible charges before the threshold
BB) $6,250 deductible; 60% of most eligible charges before the threshold
CC) $2,500 deductible; 60% of most eligible charges before the threshold
DD) $8,000 deductible; 80% of most eligible charges before the threshold
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) specifies that for non-Toronto Tempo players, out-of-network services carry a $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual and coverage for 60% of most eligible charges (and 70% for certain other eligible charges) until the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person out-of-pocket, after which coverage reaches 100%.
Q9Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2029 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) expressly provides that for the 2029 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2028 Salary Cap Year—distinct from the 12% used in 2028 and the 12.5% used in 2030.
Q10Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, if the WNBA or a Team imposes the maximum 2.5%-per-game fine on a player for missing Regular Season games and also imposes a suspension for those same missed Regular Season games, the 20% aggregate Regular Season fine cap expressly limits the total combined salary reduction from both the fines and the suspension.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 expressly states that 'nothing in this Section 7 shall be construed to limit a Team's right … to impose a suspension or other discipline on a player for the same (or other) missed games in accordance with this Agreement (and the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension).' The aggregate caps apply only to the Section 7 fines, not to salary lost from a suspension imposed for the same games.
