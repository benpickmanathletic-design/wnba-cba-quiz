Q1Art. VII §1(a)4-choice
Under the 2026 CBA, what is the Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year?
AA) $5,000,000
BB) $6,000,000
CC) $7,000,000
DD) $8,000,000
CBA Reference
Art. VII Section 1(a) explicitly states that the Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year shall be $7,000,000. This is the fixed starting point from which subsequent years are calculated.
Q2Art. VII §1(a)4-choice
Under Art. VII, what is the maximum percentage by which the Salary Cap for the 2027 Salary Cap Year can increase or decrease from the 2026 Salary Cap?
AA) 10%
BB) 12%
CC) 13%
DD) 15%
CBA Reference
Art. VII Section 1(a) states that the Salary Cap for the 2027 Salary Cap Year shall not increase or decrease by more than thirteen percent (13%) from the Salary Cap for the 2026 Salary Cap Year. For all subsequent years the cap is limited to 10%.
Q3Art. VII §1(b)4-choice
Under Art. VII, what triggers a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' that the WNBA must address through Guarantee Payments?
AA) Total Salaries fall below the league minimum threshold
BB) The Salary Cap would have increased beyond the applicable cap increase limit but for the operation of the cap ceiling provision
CC) A team fails to spend to the Minimum Team Salary
DD) Player benefits exceed the Salary Cap Benefits estimate
CBA Reference
Art. VII Section 1(b) defines 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' as the additional amount by which the Salary Cap would have increased but for the cap ceiling provision, multiplied by the number of Teams. This protects players when strong revenue growth is artificially restrained by the cap increase limits.
Q4Art. V §34-choice
Under Art. V Section 3, which of the following players is NOT permitted to negotiate allowable amendments to the Standard Player Contract?
AA) A Veteran Player in her third WNBA season
BB) A player who was a Restricted Free Agent before signing
CC) A Rookie entering her first WNBA contract
DD) A Core Player renegotiating her deal
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 3 explicitly states that only a Veteran Player (but not a Rookie) and a Team may amend the provisions of a Standard Player Contract in the specified allowable ways. Rookies are categorically excluded from negotiating amendments.
Q5Art. V §3(b)4-choice
Under Art. V Section 3(b), salary protection in a Standard Player Contract can be negotiated for which of the following events?
AA) Contract termination due to trade refusal only
BB) Termination due to lack of skill, basketball-related injury, or in-Season injury or illness
CC) Termination due to disciplinary action by the Team
DD) Termination due to a player's voluntary retirement
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 3(b) allows veteran players to negotiate salary protection in Exhibit 2 of the SPC covering termination due to: lack of skill, basketball-related injury, or disability resulting from an in-Season injury or illness, among other enumerated reasons.
Q6Art. V §2(d)4-choice
Under Art. V Section 2(d), a player may unilaterally modify her Standard Player Contract by doing which of the following?
AA) Adding performance bonus provisions
BB) Deleting paragraph 6(d) of the SPC in its entirety
CC) Modifying the Base Salary schedule in Exhibit 1
DD) Inserting a no-trade clause
CBA Reference
Art. V Section 2(d) states that notwithstanding the general prohibition on amendments, a player may unilaterally change the form of the Standard Player Contract by deleting paragraph 6(d) thereof in its entirety. This is the only unilateral modification right explicitly granted to players.
Q7Art. VI §1(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI Section 1(b), which type of free agent is subject to a Right of First Refusal?
AA) Unrestricted Free Agent
BB) Core Player
CC) Restricted Free Agent
DD) Reserved Player
CBA Reference
Art. VI Section 1(b) states that no Right of First Refusal shall be applicable to any Free Agent other than a Restricted Free Agent. All other free agent categories are explicitly exempt from ROFR obligations.
Q8Art. VI §1(a)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. VI Section 1(a), when can a Restricted Free Agent first begin negotiating an Offer Sheet with a team other than her Prior Team?
AA) January 1
BB) February 4
CC) The day following the last day of the Moratorium Period
DD) March 19
CBA Reference
Art. VI Section 1(a)(iii) states that a Restricted Free Agent is free at any time beginning on February 4 to negotiate an Offer Sheet with any Team other than her Prior Team. She may not enter into the Offer Sheet until after the Moratorium Period ends.
Q9Art. XIV §1T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV Section 1, a player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another team is explicitly identified as being in violation of her player conduct obligations.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV Section 1 explicitly states, 'For avoidance of doubt, any player who publicly expresses a desire to be traded to another Team shall be in violation of this Section 1.' This is a notable specific conduct provision in the 2026 CBA.
Q10Art. X §1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. X Section 1, a player whose Standard Player Contract is terminated before the Mid-Point of the Regular Season continues to receive medical benefits until the end of the calendar year.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. X Section 1(a) distinguishes between two scenarios: a player terminated before the Mid-Point receives medical benefits for only thirty (30) days following termination, while a player terminated after the Mid-Point receives benefits until the end of the calendar year.
