Q1Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection is extended. During which specific period does the exception to the seven (7) Player Contract Base Salary protection limit apply, and at precisely what point does it cease?
AA) The exception applies during the initial three Seasons only; it ceases at the start of the option year.
BB) The exception applies during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract (initial term plus option year); it ceases once the extended term of the Extension begins.
CC) The exception applies during the entire life of the Contract including the extended term; it never ceases unless the Contract is terminated.
DD) The exception applies only during the first two Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract; it ceases when the option for the fourth Season is exercised.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) expressly states that the exception applies 'with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract' and 'shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' Thus the exception ceases precisely when the extended term begins.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is INELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player who has exactly six (6) Years of Service at the time she signs the Contract.
BB) A player who has seven (7) Years of Service at the time she signs the Extension.
CC) A player who has five (5) Years of Service at the time she signs the Contract.
DD) A player who has eight (8) Years of Service at the time she signs the Contract.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) provides that no Contract or Extension entered into by a player who, at the time of signing, has FEWER than six (6) Years of Service may contain a trade bonus. A player with exactly five (5) Years of Service has fewer than six and is therefore ineligible.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv), a Contract that does NOT contain a trade bonus is extended. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the earliest point at which the trade bonus added at extension first becomes payable?
AA) The bonus is payable the first time the Contract is traded, regardless of when that trade occurs.
BB) The bonus is payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term of the Extension.
CC) The bonus is payable only the second time the Contract is traded after the extension is signed.
DD) The bonus is payable immediately upon execution of the Extension if the Contract is then traded within 48 hours.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv) states that when a trade bonus is added to a Contract at extension, it 'shall be payable only the first time such Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term of the Extension,' and Exhibit 8 must include the required bracketed-season language to that effect.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 5(b); Art. I (Qualifying Offer)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), which of the following players qualifies for a Restricted Qualifying Offer, and per Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' what is the salary floor for that offer?
AA) A Veteran Free Agent with three (3) Years of Service; salary floor is the applicable Minimum Player Salary.
BB) A Veteran Free Agent with four (4) or more Years of Service; salary floor is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of her prior Contract.
CC) Any Veteran Free Agent regardless of Years of Service; salary floor is 105% of her prior year Base Salary.
DD) A Veteran Free Agent with four (4) or more Years of Service; salary floor is always 105% of her prior year Base Salary.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) limits Restricted Qualifying Offer eligibility to Veteran Free Agents with four (4) or more Years of Service. Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' (subsection D) provides that the salary floor for such a Restricted Qualifying Offer is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
Q5Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Guarantee Shortfall' for a given Salary Cap Year is computed as the difference between which two figures?
AA) The Minimum Team Salary multiplied by the number of Teams, minus Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year.
BB) The Salary Cap for the Salary Cap Year multiplied by the total number of Teams in the WNBA that Season, minus Total Salaries for such Salary Cap Year.
CC) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year minus the Designated 20% Share of SBR for such Salary Cap Year.
DD) The sum of all Salary Cap Limit Shortfalls for prior years, minus Total Salaries for the current Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) states that the 'Guarantee Shortfall' equals the amount by which Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year is less than the Salary Cap for such Salary Cap Year multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in such Season.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(c)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), what percentage of the Salary Cap constitutes the Minimum Team Salary, and which of the following is the recognized exception to the WNBA's obligation to cause a Team to make shortfall payments?
AA) 80%; a Team that loses more than ten (10) players to injury in a Season is exempt.
BB) 85%; a Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season is not required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
CC) 90%; a Team in its first expansion Season is exempt from the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
DD) 85%; a Team whose Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary due to a Salary Cap Limit Shortfall is exempt.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(i) sets the Minimum Team Salary at eighty-five percent (85%) of the Salary Cap. Sec. 1(c)(ii) expressly provides that any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season shall not be required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states BOTH the in-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the out-of-pocket threshold at which in-network coverage reaches 100%?
AA) $600 in-network deductible; 100% coverage after covered individual has paid $5,000 per person in-network.

CC) $600 in-network deductible; 100% coverage after covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in-network.

Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) states that for non-Toronto Tempo players, in-network services carry a $600 annual deductible per covered individual, with coverage at 80% until the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year, and 100% thereafter.
Q8Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, a player with a Base Salary of $250,000 misses ten (10) Regular Season games in a Season. Which of the following correctly states the MAXIMUM aggregate fine the Team may impose for those missed Regular Season games?
AA) $62,500 (10 games × 2.5% × $250,000), subject to the 20% aggregate cap of $50,000.
BB) $62,500, because the per-game cap of 2.5% applies but there is no aggregate cap for missing more than eight games.
CC) $50,000, which is 20% of $250,000, because the aggregate cap is hit before the per-game calculation reaches its full amount.
DD) $25,000, because only five (5) games' worth of fines may be imposed in any Season.
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 allows fines of up to 2.5% of Base Salary per missed Regular Season game, but caps aggregate Regular Season fines at 20% of Base Salary. Ten games at 2.5% = 25%, which exceeds the 20% cap; therefore the maximum is 20% × $250,000 = $50,000.

Q10Art. XI Sec. 1(a)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2031 Season and beyond, the CBA expressly provides that all non-Developmental Players, regardless of Base Salary level, are entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of their WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a) only expressly addresses housing entitlements through the 2030 Season (all non-Developmental Players for 2026-2028; those with Base Salary ≤$500,000 for 2029-2030). The CBA makes no express provision for Team-provided apartments beyond the 2030 Season, so the statement is false.
