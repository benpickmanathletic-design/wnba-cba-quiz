Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(iv)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv), a Player Contract that does NOT already contain a trade bonus may be amended to add one only under what specific circumstance, and what restriction applies to when that added bonus is payable?
AA) Only if the player has 6+ Years of Service at the time of amendment; the bonus is payable only the first time the Contract is traded after the amendment date.
BB) Only if the Contract is extended simultaneously; the bonus is payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the extended term of the Extension.
CC) Only if the Contract is assigned to a new Team; the bonus is payable only the first time the Contract is traded following such assignment.
DD) Only if the player and Team mutually agree in writing during the Moratorium Period; the bonus is payable at any time the Contract is traded after the agreement.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(iv) states a Contract without a trade bonus may be amended to add one only if the Contract is simultaneously extended, and the added bonus is payable only the first time the Contract is traded following the Season immediately preceding the first Season of the extended term. Exhibit 8 must include the specified language identifying that preceding Season.
Q2Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, which of the following CORRECTLY states the Base Salary protection rule for the SECOND Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the second Season is fully protected from the moment the Contract is signed.
BB) The Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CC) The Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected only if the option for the fourth Season is exercised by the Team.
DD) The Base Salary for the second Season is never protected under any Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round or Third Round Pick.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii) provides that for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, the Base Salary for the second Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the second Regular Season covered by such Contract. The first Season expressly may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind.
Q3Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii), the exception allowing a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection to NOT count against a Team's per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts with Base Salary protection ceases to apply at what precise point?
AA) When the player's Rookie Scale Contract is terminated for any reason.
BB) When the option for the fourth Season of the Rookie Scale Contract is exercised by the Team.
CC) When the Rookie Scale Contract is extended, i.e., the exception does not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any such Contract.
DD) When the player's Base Salary is renegotiated pursuant to Art. VII, Sec. 5(c)(ii), removing conditional protections.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) expressly states the exception 'shall only apply with respect to and during the four Seasons of a Rookie Scale Contract and shall not apply with respect to or during the extended term of any Rookie Scale Contract that has been extended.' Once the Rookie Scale Contract is extended, the exception ceases and any Base Salary protection counts against the seven-Contract limit.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team designates Player A as a Core Player. She plays three (3) Seasons under the Contract signed while designated but ZERO Seasons under any Extension of that Contract. The Team later designates Player B as a Core Player and she plays two (2) Seasons under her Contract and one (1) Season under an Extension of that Contract. How many total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) 5 Seasons (3 from Player A + 2 from Player B; Extension Seasons are excluded for both players).
BB) 6 Seasons (3 from Player A + 3 from Player B including her Extension Season).
CC) 5 Seasons (3 from Player A + 2 from Player B; the Extension Season for Player B is excluded).
DD) 4 Seasons (3 from Player A + 1 from Player B; only the first Season of each player's Contract counts).
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) states that Seasons played under the Contract signed while the player was designated (but NOT including Seasons covered by any Extension) count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit. For Player A, 3 Seasons count; for Player B, only 2 Seasons under her original Contract count — the 1 Extension Season is excluded. Total: 5 Seasons.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b); Art. I (Qualifying Offer)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) and Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' a Veteran Free Agent with exactly six (6) Years of Service receives a Restricted Qualifying Offer from her Prior Team. Which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) 105% of the player's Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract.
BB) The Standard Maximum Salary applicable to the Salary Cap Year of the offer.
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
DD) The Supermax Salary applicable to the Salary Cap Year of the offer.
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' clause (D) provides that for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), the Restricted Qualifying Offer floor is 'the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.' This applies regardless of whether the player has 4, 6, or any other qualifying number of Years of Service under Sec. 5(b).
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii), the 'Guarantee Shortfall' for a Salary Cap Year is triggered and computed under which of the following conditions?
AA) When Total Salaries for a Salary Cap Year exceed the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams; the shortfall equals the excess amount.
BB) When, as of the conclusion of a Season, Total Salaries for that Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap for that Year multiplied by the total number of Teams in that Season; the shortfall equals that difference.
CC) When any individual Team's Team Salary falls below 85% of the Salary Cap; the shortfall equals the difference between the Team Salary and the Minimum Team Salary.
DD) When the Salary Cap would have increased by more than 10% but for the cap-smoothing rule; the shortfall equals the excess increase multiplied by the number of Teams.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(ii) states that if, as of the conclusion of a Season, Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap for that Year multiplied by the total number of Teams participating in the WNBA in that Season, the 'Guarantee Shortfall' equals that shortfall amount. This is distinct from the Salary Cap Limit Shortfall under Sec. 1(b)(i), which addresses the cap-smoothing mechanism.
Q7Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states BOTH the in-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the out-of-pocket threshold at which in-network coverage reaches 100%?
AA) $600 in-network deductible per individual; 100% coverage after the covered individual has paid $8,000 per person in a calendar year.
BB) $2,500 in-network deductible per individual; 100% coverage after the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year.
CC) $600 in-network deductible per individual; 100% coverage after the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year.
DD) $1,800 in-network deductible per individual; 100% coverage after the covered individual has paid $9,000 per person in a calendar year.
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) provides that for non-Toronto Tempo players, in-network coverage includes a $600 annual deductible per covered individual ($1,800 per family) and coverage at 80% until the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person ($9,000 per family) in a calendar year, with 100% coverage thereafter. The $2,500 deductible and $8,000 out-of-pocket threshold apply to out-of-network services.
Q8Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2031 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year.
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year.
CC) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2030 Salary Cap Year.
DD) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2031 Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) expressly provides that for each of the 2031 and 2032 Salary Cap Years, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the Salary Cap Year immediately preceding the applicable Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the Salary Cap Year immediately preceding the applicable Salary Cap Year. For the 2031 Salary Cap Year, that means 30.5% of 2030 League Revenue plus 13% of 2030 Team Revenue.
Q9Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), the Rookie Scale Amounts for Second Round Picks and Third Round Picks in Rookie Scale Contracts commencing with Seasons after 2026 are adjusted upward by a fixed 4% increase each Season, regardless of any change in the Salary Cap.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) states that for Rookie Scale Contracts commencing with each Season following the 2026 Season, Second Round Pick and Third Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by increasing the immediately prior Season's amounts by 4% — a fixed 4% increase — rather than by the percentage change in the Salary Cap that applies to First Round Picks. This is a distinct mechanism from the First Round Pick adjustment.
Q10Art. XIV Sec. 7T/F
True or False: Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, if a Team (or the WNBA) imposes a 2.5% per-game fine on a player for missing Regular Season games AND also imposes a suspension for the same missed games, the 20% aggregate Regular Season fine cap limits the total combined salary reduction from both the fines and the suspension.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 expressly states that 'the 20% and 25% caps noted above shall not be applicable to any lost salary due to any such suspension.' Accordingly, while the 20% cap limits aggregate Section 7 fines for missed Regular Season games, it does NOT cap or limit any additional salary reduction resulting from a suspension imposed for the same (or other) missed games.
