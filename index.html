Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(i)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g), which of the following statements correctly describes the operation of a trade bonus when a Contract is signed in connection with an agreement to trade the Contract under Art. V, Sec. 3(f) AND the Contract contains a trade bonus?
AA) The trade bonus is payable on the initial trade pursuant to the Sec. 3(f) agreement, as originally specified.
BB) The trade bonus is void entirely and cannot be enforced on any subsequent trade.
CC) The trade bonus does not apply to the initial Sec. 3(f) trade but is instead payable only the second time the Contract is traded.
DD) The trade bonus is split equally between the initial trade and the second trade.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(i) provides that if a Contract is signed in connection with an agreement to trade under Sec. 3(f) and contains a trade bonus, the bonus shall not apply to that initial trade but shall instead be payable only the second time the Contract is traded. This is a specific carve-out from the general rule that a trade bonus is payable only the first time a Contract is traded.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(b)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), which of the following correctly describes the treatment of a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection WITH RESPECT TO the seven (7) Player Contract limit, and what ADDITIONAL restriction applies once that Rookie Scale Contract is extended?
AA) A Rookie Scale Contract with Base Salary protection never counts against the seven-contract limit, even during the extended term of an Extension.
BB) A Rookie Scale Contract with Base Salary protection does not count against the seven-contract limit during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract, but DOES count against the limit during the extended term of any Extension of that Contract.
CC) A Rookie Scale Contract with Base Salary protection always counts against the seven-contract limit from the first Season onward.
DD) A Rookie Scale Contract with Base Salary protection counts against the limit only in the third and fourth Seasons of the Contract.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b)(iii) states that a Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection does not count against the per-team limit of seven contracts, but this exception applies only during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract and expressly does NOT apply during the extended term of any Extension of such Contract. Once extended, the contract counts against the seven-contract cap.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick, which of the following MOST accurately describes the full Base Salary protection schedule across all Seasons of the Contract?
AA) No Base Salary protection for the first Season; second Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested before the first day of the second Regular Season; third and fourth Seasons become fully protected if the fourth-Season option is exercised.
BB) First Season is fully protected upon signing; second Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested before the first day of the second Regular Season.
CC) No Base Salary protection of any kind for the first or second Season; third and fourth Seasons become fully protected if the fourth-Season option is exercised.
DD) All Seasons are unprotected unless the Team and player agree to Exhibit 2 protections outside the Rookie Scale framework.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii) explicitly states that a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round or Third Round Pick may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season; the second Season becomes fully protected only if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the second Regular Season; and the third and fourth Seasons become fully protected only if the fourth-Season option is exercised.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), which of the following correctly states the maximum number of Core Player designations a Team may make AND the treatment of Seasons played under an Extension of a Core Player Contract toward that maximum?
AA) A Team may designate a maximum of two (2) Core Players at any one time; Seasons played under both the base Contract and any Extension count toward the maximum.
BB) A Team may designate no more than one (1) Core Player; only Seasons covered by the original Contract signed while the player was designated (not Seasons of any Extension) count toward the maximum number of times a player may be designated by that Team.
CC) A Team may designate no more than one (1) Core Player; all Seasons including the Extension count toward the maximum.
DD) A Team may designate no more than two (2) Core Players; only the first two Seasons of the Core Player Contract count toward the maximum.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) provides that each Team may designate no more than one (1) Veteran Free Agent as a Core Player, and that only Seasons played pursuant to the Contract signed while the player was designated (but NOT Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract) are included in calculating the maximum number of designations. This distinction between base Contract Seasons and Extension Seasons is a key nuance.
Q5Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent must have how many Years of Service to be eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer, and what is the correct salary floor for that offer under the CBA definitions?
AA) Three (3) Years of Service; the floor is the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary in the last Season of the prior Contract, whichever is greater.
BB) Four (4) Years of Service; the floor is the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
CC) Four (4) Years of Service; the floor is one hundred five percent (105%) of the Base Salary in the last Season of the prior Contract.
DD) Five (5) Years of Service; the floor is the Standard Maximum Salary.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 5(b) states that any Veteran Free Agent who has four (4) Years of Service is eligible to receive a Restricted Qualifying Offer. The definition of 'Qualifying Offer' in Art. I confirms that a Restricted Qualifying Offer for such players must equal the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.
Q6Art. VII Sec. 1(c)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(c), if a Team's Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary at the conclusion of a Season, which of the following correctly describes the WNBA's obligation and a notable exception to that obligation?
AA) The WNBA must fine the Team an amount equal to the shortfall; no exceptions apply.
BB) The WNBA shall cause the Team to make payments equal to the shortfall distributed to that Team's players; however, any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a WNBA Season is not required to meet the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
CC) The WNBA shall redistribute the shortfall equally to all WNBA players leaguewide, and no team-specific exception applies.
DD) The WNBA shall carry the shortfall forward to the following Salary Cap Year and apply it against that Team's cap space; no exception applies for ceasing operations.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(c)(ii) requires the WNBA to cause any Team whose Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary (85% of the cap) to make payments equal to the shortfall, distributed to that Team's players. The provision includes an explicit carve-out: any Team that ceases operations prior to the conclusion of a Season is not required to satisfy the Minimum Team Salary obligation.
Q7Art. VIII Sec. (b) and (c)4-choice
Under Art. VIII, Sec. (b) and (c), what is the Rookie Scale Amount for the overall first pick in the 2026 WNBA Draft for the FIRST Season of her Rookie Scale Contract, and how are Rookie Scale Amounts for SECOND Round Picks adjusted for Seasons after 2026?
AA) $500,000 for the first pick's first Season; Second Round Pick amounts are adjusted by the same percentage as the Salary Cap increase (or decrease).
BB) $520,000 for the first pick's first Season; Second Round Pick amounts are increased by a fixed 4% annually.
CC) $500,000 for the first pick's first Season; Second Round Pick amounts are increased by a fixed 4% annually regardless of Salary Cap movement.
DD) $466,913 for the first pick's first Season; Second Round Pick amounts mirror First Round Pick adjustments exactly.
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (b) sets the first Season Rookie Scale Amount for overall Pick 1 at $500,000. Art. VIII, Sec. (c) draws a critical distinction: First Round Pick amounts adjust by the percentage change in the Salary Cap, while Second Round (and Third Round) Pick amounts are increased by a fixed 4% annually, regardless of whether the Salary Cap increases by more or less than that amount.
Q8Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, a Team (or the WNBA if the Team declines) fines a player for missing both Regular Season and playoff games in the same Season. Which of the following statements about the interaction of the two fine caps is CORRECT?
AA) The 20% Regular Season cap and the 25% playoff cap are combined into a single 45% aggregate cap for the Season.
BB) The 20% Regular Season aggregate cap and the 25% playoff aggregate cap operate independently of each other; the 20% and 25% caps do not apply to lost salary resulting from any suspension imposed for the same missed games.
CC) Whichever cap is lower (20%) governs all missed-game fines in that Season.
DD) The playoff fine cap of 25% supersedes and replaces the Regular Season cap of 20% once any playoff game is missed.
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 establishes separate aggregate caps of 20% of Base Salary for missed Regular Season games and 25% for missed playoff games in any Season. It also expressly provides that these percentage caps do not apply to any lost salary resulting from a suspension separately imposed for the same (or other) missed games, meaning a Team may still suspend the player in addition to the fine without the 20%/25% caps limiting the suspension amount.
Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, ALL players who are not Developmental Players are entitled to Team-provided one-bedroom apartments in the home city of their WNBA Team, regardless of their Base Salary level.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
This statement is FALSE. Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) limits the one-bedroom apartment entitlement for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons to non-Developmental Players whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000 during the corresponding Season. Players earning more than $500,000 in those Seasons are NOT entitled to Team-provided housing under this provision.
Q10Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' formula for the 2028 Salary Cap Year uses 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
This statement is FALSE. Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) specifies that for the 2028 Salary Cap Year, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year plus 12% (not 12.25%) of Team Revenue for the 2027 Salary Cap Year. The 12.25% rate does not apply until the 2029 Salary Cap Year.
