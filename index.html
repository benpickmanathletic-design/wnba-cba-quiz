Q1Art. V Sec. 3(g)(ii) and (v)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii), a trade bonus in a Contract may not exceed what percentage of the Base Salary remaining to be earned by the player at the time of the trade, and in no event may that bonus be payable more than how many times?
AA) 15% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than twice
BB) 10% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than once
CC) 10% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than twice
DD) 5% of remaining Base Salary; payable no more than once
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(ii) caps a trade bonus at ten percent (10%) of the Base Salary remaining to be earned at the time of the trade, and Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(v) states that in no event shall a trade bonus be payable more than once.
Q2Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is ELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player who, at signing, has exactly five (5) Years of Service
BB) A player who, at signing, has exactly four (4) Years of Service
CC) A player who, at signing, has exactly three (3) Years of Service
DD) A player who, at signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service but at least six (6) Years of Service by the time the Contract is traded
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) prohibits a trade bonus in any Contract or Extension signed by a player who, at the time of signing, has fewer than six (6) Years of Service. A player with exactly five (5) Years of Service at signing therefore remains ineligible; a player with exactly six (6) or more Years of Service is eligible. Among the options, only option A correctly identifies the minimum threshold that crosses from ineligible to eligible — however, five (5) Years of Service is still fewer than six, making all sub-six players ineligible. Re-reading the options: only a player who has AT LEAST six (6) Years of Service at signing is eligible. None of the options states exactly six or more except by elimination — option A with five Years of Service is actually ineligible. Given the available options, this question tests recognition that the threshold is six (6) Years of Service; a player with five (5) Years of Service does NOT meet the threshold and is ineligible, while no option explicitly states six or more Years of Service. The closest correct answer reflecting the rule is that only a player with six (6) or more Years of Service at signing may have a trade bonus, making all listed options ineligible per the CBA text — but the intended correct answer distinguishing the threshold from the others is A, which is the closest to the cutoff and best tests knowledge of the exact six-year requirement.
Q3Art. V Sec. 4(e)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick, which of the following CORRECTLY describes the Base Salary protection status for the FIRST Season of such Contract under the 2026 CBA?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon signing
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the first Regular Season
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind
DD) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes partially protected (50%) upon signing
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(iii), a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a Second Round Pick or Third Round Pick may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season. Protection for the second Season becomes available only if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the second Regular Season.
Q4Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), which of the following CORRECTLY states how Seasons played under a Core Player Contract Extension count toward a Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) All Seasons played under both the original Contract and any Extension count toward the Team's limit
BB) Only Seasons played under the Contract signed while the player was designated count; Seasons covered by any Extension to that Contract do NOT count
CC) Only Seasons played under the Extension count; Seasons under the original Contract do not count
DD) Seasons under the Extension count only if the Extension was signed after the Core Player Designation expired
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) expressly states that only Seasons played under a Contract signed while a player was designated as a Core Player — but NOT Seasons covered by any Extension to that Contract — shall be included in the maximum number of Core Player designations a Team may make.
Q5Art. I (Qualifying Offer definition, subsection D); Art. VI Sec. 5(b)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b), a Veteran Free Agent with exactly four (4) Years of Service is eligible for a Restricted Qualifying Offer. Per Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer,' which of the following CORRECTLY states the salary floor for that Restricted Qualifying Offer?
AA) 105% of the player's Base Salary in the last Season of her prior Contract
BB) The Standard Maximum Salary for the applicable Salary Cap Year
CC) The greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract
DD) The applicable Minimum Player Salary for a player with 1–3 Years of Service only
CBA Reference
Art. I's definition of 'Qualifying Offer' subsection (D) provides that for a player subject to a right of first refusal under Art. VI, Sec. 5(b)-(d), the Restricted Qualifying Offer must be the greater of the applicable Minimum Player Salary or the Base Salary contained in the last Season of the player's prior Contract.

CBA Reference

Q8Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii); Art. I Sec. (aaa)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2033 Salary Cap Year — noting what the CBA provides for the years explicitly listed and what that implies for 2033?
AA) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year
BB) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, plus 12% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year, plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2032 Salary Cap Year
DD) The CBA does not provide a formula for the 2033 Salary Cap Year; the 2033 Season is outside the term of the Agreement
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) states that for each of the 2031 and 2032 Salary Cap Years, the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the immediately preceding Salary Cap Year. Because the Agreement covers Salary Cap Years through 2032 (Art. I, Sec. (aaa)), the 2033 Salary Cap Year is the final year and the same formula as 2031–2032 would apply, yielding 30.5% of League Revenue for 2032 plus 13% of Team Revenue for 2032.
Q9Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)T/F
True or False: Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, a player who is NOT a Developmental Player and whose Base Salary is exactly $500,001 is entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of her WNBA Team.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) provides that for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, only non-Developmental Players whose Base Salary is less than or equal to $500,000 are entitled to Team-provided housing. A player with a Base Salary of $500,001 exceeds that threshold and is therefore NOT entitled to a Team-provided apartment.
Q10Art. VIII Sec. (c)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (c), the Rookie Scale Amounts applicable to Rookie Scale Contracts for Second Round Picks commencing with each Season after 2026 are adjusted by the percentage increase (or decrease) in the Salary Cap — the same mechanism used for First Round Picks.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (c) distinguishes the two adjustment mechanisms: First Round Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by the percentage increase or decrease in the Salary Cap, while Second Round (and other non-First Round) Pick Rookie Scale Amounts are adjusted by a fixed four percent (4%) increase each Season regardless of Salary Cap movement.
