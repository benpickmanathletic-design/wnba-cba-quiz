Q1Art. VII s1(a)4-choice
Under Article VII, Section 1(a), what is the 2026 Salary Cap, and by what maximum percentage can the 2027 Salary Cap increase or decrease from it?
AA) $6,000,000; capped at 10% change
BB) $7,000,000; capped at 13% change
CC) $7,000,000; capped at 10% change
DD) $8,000,000; capped at 13% change
CBA Reference
Article VII, Section 1(a)(i) sets the 2026 Salary Cap at exactly $7,000,000. Section 1(a)(ii) provides that the 2027 Salary Cap cannot increase or decrease by more than 13% from the 2026 figure, while all subsequent years are capped at 10%.
Q2Art. VII s1(c)4-choice
Under Article VII, Section 1(c), what percentage of the Salary Cap constitutes the Minimum Team Salary, and what happens if a Team falls short of that threshold at season's end?
AA) 75%; the Team forfeits draft picks
BB) 80%; the WNBA fines the Team
CC) 85%; the WNBA causes the Team to make payments equal to the shortfall disbursed to players
DD) 90%; the Team must escrow the difference for the following season
CBA Reference
Article VII, Section 1(c)(i) establishes the Minimum Team Salary at 85% of the Salary Cap. Section 1(c)(ii) requires the WNBA to cause any deficient Team to make payments equal to the shortfall to be disbursed to players on that Team.
Q3Art. V s3(b)4-choice
Under Article V, Section 3(b), what is the per-team limit on the number of Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection of any kind, and which of the following correctly states an exception to that limit?
AA) Six (6) contracts; Rookie Scale Contracts never count against the limit regardless of extension
BB) Seven (7) contracts; a Rookie Scale Contract (including during any extended term) does not count against the limit
CC) Seven (7) contracts; a Rookie Scale Contract counts against the limit only during its four initial Seasons, not during any extended term
DD) Eight (8) contracts; contracts held by Core Players are excluded from the limit
CBA Reference
Article V, Section 3(b) sets a per-team cap of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection. The exception for Rookie Scale Contracts explicitly applies only during the four Seasons of the initial contract and does NOT apply during any extended term.
Q4Art. V s3(g)4-choice
According to Article V, Section 3(g), a trade bonus in a Player Contract may not exceed what percentage of Base Salary remaining at the time of trade, and which players are categorically ineligible to have a trade bonus in their contract?
AA) 15%; players with fewer than four (4) Years of Service
BB) 10%; players with fewer than six (6) Years of Service at the time of signing
CC) 10%; players with fewer than four (4) Years of Service at the time of signing
DD) 15%; Rookie Scale Contract players only
CBA Reference
Article V, Section 3(g)(ii) caps a trade bonus at 10% of the Base Salary remaining at the time of the trade. Section 3(g)(vi) prohibits any Contract or Extension signed by a player who has fewer than six (6) Years of Service at the time of signing from containing a trade bonus.
Q5Art. V s4(e)4-choice
Under Article V, Section 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a First Round Pick selected 'in the Lottery,' when does the Base Salary for the FIRST Season become fully protected?
AA) Only if the team exercises the fourth-year option
BB) Only if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the first Regular Season
CC) It is immediately and unconditionally fully protected upon signing
DD) Only if waivers are not requested prior to the first day of the second Regular Season
CBA Reference
Article V, Section 4(e)(i) states that for a Lottery Pick's Rookie Scale Contract, the Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected at signing with no condition attached. This is a key distinction from non-Lottery First Round Picks, whose first-Season protection is conditioned on not being waived before the first day of the first Regular Season.

AA) 48 hours, excluding weekends and holidays
BB) Three (3) business days
CC) 5:00 p.m. ET on the fourth day following receipt, including weekends and holidays
DD) Five (5) business days following receipt

Article VI, Section 6(d) defines the First Refusal Exercise Notice Deadline as 5:00 p.m. ET on the fourth day following the date the ROFR Team receives the Offer Sheet, explicitly clarifying that this calculation includes weekends and holidays.
Q7Art. VI s7(b)4-choice
Under Article VI, Section 7, which of the following accurately describes a restriction on Core Player Designations applicable to the period prior to the 2026 Season?
AA) No player may be designated as a Core Player if she has more than eight (8) Years of Service
BB) No player may be designated as a Core Player if she has played pursuant to a Contract signed while she was previously designated as a Core Player by the same Team
CC) Each Team may designate up to two (2) Core Players for the 2026 Season only
DD) A Core Player designation may only be made if the Team has cap room equal to the Supermax salary
CBA Reference
Article VI, Section 7(b) explicitly states that during the Core Player Designation window prior to the 2026 Season, no player may be designated if she has played pursuant to a Contract signed while she was previously designated as a Core Player by that same Team. This was a new restriction introduced in the 2026 CBA.
Q8Art. X s1(a)4-choice
Under Article X, Section 1(a), for in-network medical coverage, what is the annual deductible per covered individual, and at what out-of-pocket threshold does coverage reach 100%?
AA) $500 deductible; 100% after $2,500 out-of-pocket
BB) $600 deductible; 100% after $3,000 out-of-pocket per person
CC) $1,000 deductible; 100% after $5,000 out-of-pocket per person
DD) $600 deductible; 100% after $8,000 out-of-pocket per person
CBA Reference
Article X, Section 1(a)(i) specifies in-network medical coverage with a $600 annual deductible per covered individual and coverage at 80% until the covered individual has paid $3,000 per person in a calendar year, after which coverage reaches 100% with no annual or lifetime maximum.
Q9Art. XIV s74-choice
Under Article XIV, Section 7, what is the maximum fine a Team or the WNBA may impose per missed Regular Season game, and what is the aggregate cap on Regular Season game-missing fines for a single Season?
AA) Up to 5% per game; capped at 25% of Base Salary
BB) Up to 2.5% per game; capped at 20% of Base Salary
CC) Up to 2.5% per game; capped at 25% of Base Salary
DD) Up to 5% per game; capped at 20% of Base Salary
CBA Reference
Article XIV, Section 7 permits fines of up to 2.5% of a player's Base Salary for each missed Regular Season game, with an aggregate cap of 20% of Base Salary for missed Regular Season games in any one Season. The 5% per game and 25% aggregate cap apply specifically to missed playoff games.
Q10Art. VIIIT/F
True or False: Under Article VIII, the 2026 Rookie Scale Amount for the overall first pick is $500,000 in Year 1, and for Second Round picks and Third Round picks the Year 1 Rookie Scale Amount is identical at $270,000.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Article VIII's Rookie Scale table confirms that the first overall pick receives $500,000 in Year 1 of a Rookie Scale Contract commencing in 2026, and that both Second Round and Third Round picks have identical Year 1 amounts of $270,000. The statement is therefore true.
