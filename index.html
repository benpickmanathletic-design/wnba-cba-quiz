Q1Art. V Sec. 4(e)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e), for a Rookie Scale Contract signed by a non-Lottery First Round Pick, which of the following correctly describes the Base Salary protection status for the FIRST Season of such Contract?
AA) The Base Salary for the first Season is fully protected upon signing, identical to a Lottery pick.
BB) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season covered by such Contract.
CC) The Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if the Team exercises the fourth-year option.
DD) Such a Rookie Scale Contract may not contain Base Salary protection of any kind for the first Season.
CBA Reference
Under Art. V, Sec. 4(e)(ii), for a non-Lottery First Round Pick, the Base Salary for the first Season becomes fully protected only if waivers are not requested on the player prior to the first day of the first Regular Season—unlike a Lottery pick whose first Season salary is immediately fully protected upon signing.
Q2Art. XII Sec. 1(a)(ii)4-choice
Under Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii), which of the following correctly states the formula for the 'Designated 20% Share of SBR' for the 2030 Salary Cap Year?
AA) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.25% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year.
BB) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year.
CC) 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 13% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year.
DD) 30.25% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year.
CBA Reference
Art. XII, Sec. 1(a)(ii) expressly states that for the 2030 Salary Cap Year the Designated 20% Share of SBR equals 30.5% of League Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year plus 12.5% of Team Revenue for the 2029 Salary Cap Year—distinct from the 2029 formula (which uses 12.25%) and the 2031–2032 formula (which uses 13%).
Q3Art. VII Sec. 1(b)(i)4-choice
Under Art. VII, Sec. 1(b), a 'Salary Cap Limit Shortfall' for a given Salary Cap Year (other than 2027) arises when which of the following conditions is met?
AA) The Salary Cap for that year would have increased by more than 10% over the prior year's Salary Cap but for the cap-increase limitation, with the Shortfall equal to the excess amount multiplied by the number of Teams.
BB) A Team's Team Salary falls below the Minimum Team Salary at the conclusion of the Season.
CC) Total Salaries for the Salary Cap Year are less than the Salary Cap multiplied by the total number of Teams.
DD) The WNBA fails to distribute League Shortfall payments within sixty (60) days of notice.
CBA Reference
Art. VII, Sec. 1(b)(i) defines a Salary Cap Limit Shortfall as arising when the Salary Cap would have increased by more than 10% (or 13% for 2027) but for Sec. 1(a)(ii)'s cap, with the Shortfall equal to the excess increase multiplied by the number of Teams. Option C describes a Guarantee Shortfall under Sec. 1(b)(ii), which is a separate concept.
Q4Art. V Sec. 3(g)(vi)4-choice
Under Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi), which of the following players is ELIGIBLE to have a trade bonus included in her Contract or Extension under the 2026 CBA?
AA) A player who has exactly five (5) Years of Service at the time she signs a new Contract.
BB) A player who has exactly four (4) Years of Service at the time she signs a new Extension.
CC) A player who has exactly six (6) Years of Service at the time she signs a new Contract.
DD) A player who has three (3) Years of Service but whose prior Contract already contained a trade bonus.
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(g)(vi) prohibits any Contract or Extension entered into by a player who has FEWER THAN six (6) Years of Service from containing a trade bonus, meaning a player with exactly six (6) Years of Service meets the minimum threshold and is eligible.
Q5Art. XI Sec. 1(a)(iii)4-choice
Under Art. XI, Sec. 1(a), for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, which of the following players IS entitled to a Team-provided one-bedroom apartment in the home city of her WNBA Team?
AA) A non-Developmental player whose Base Salary is $500,001 for the corresponding Season.
BB) A non-Developmental player whose Base Salary is exactly $500,000 for the corresponding Season.
CC) A non-Developmental player whose Base Salary is $750,000 for the corresponding Season.
DD) A Developmental Player whose Base Salary is $270,000 for the corresponding Season.
CBA Reference
Art. XI, Sec. 1(a)(iii) provides that for the 2029 and 2030 Seasons, non-Developmental players whose Base Salary is 'less than or equal to $500,000' receive Team-provided housing, so a player at exactly $500,000 qualifies. A player above $500,000 does not, and Developmental Players are excluded from the provision entirely.
Q6Art. XIV Sec. 74-choice
Under Art. XIV, Sec. 7, a Team seeks to fine a player for missing five (5) Regular Season games in a Season. The player's Base Salary is $400,000. Which of the following correctly states the MAXIMUM aggregate fine the Team may impose for those missed Regular Season games?
AA) $50,000 (5 games × 2.5% × $400,000).
BB) $80,000 (20% × $400,000), because the per-game amounts (5 × $10,000 = $50,000) do not exceed the 20% cap.
CC) $100,000 (25% × $400,000).
DD) $10,000, because the per-game fine is capped at a flat $2,000 per game regardless of salary.
CBA Reference
Art. XIV, Sec. 7 permits fines of up to 2.5% of Base Salary per Regular Season game missed, with an aggregate cap of 20% of Base Salary for the Season. Five games at 2.5% of $400,000 = $10,000 per game × 5 = $50,000, which is below the 20% ($80,000) aggregate cap, so $50,000 is the applicable maximum for those five games.
Q7Art. VI Sec. 7(a)4-choice
Under Art. VI, Sec. 7(a), a Team has already made Core Player designations such that players have played a combined total of two (2) Seasons under Contracts signed while they were designated. One of those players' Contracts was then extended and she played two (2) additional Seasons under the extended term. How many of those four total Seasons count toward the Team's maximum Core Player designation limit?
AA) All four (4) Seasons count, because all Seasons played under or following a Core Player designation are included.
BB) Only two (2) Seasons count—the Seasons played under the original Core Player Contract—because Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract are expressly excluded.
CC) Three (3) Seasons count, because only one Extension Season is excluded.
DD) None of the Seasons count until the player becomes an Unrestricted Free Agent.
CBA Reference
Art. VI, Sec. 7(a) states that Seasons played 'while such player was so designated (but not including Seasons covered by any Extension to such Contract) shall be included in the maximum number' of Core Player designations. Accordingly, only the two original Seasons count; the two Extension Seasons are expressly excluded.
Q8Art. X Sec. 1(a)(i)4-choice
Under Art. X, Sec. 1(a), for a non-Toronto Tempo player, which of the following correctly states BOTH the in-network annual deductible per covered individual AND the out-of-network annual deductible per covered individual under the WNBA medical plan?
AA) In-network: $600; Out-of-network: $2,500.
BB) In-network: $1,800; Out-of-network: $6,250.
CC) In-network: $600; Out-of-network: $8,000.
DD) In-network: $3,000; Out-of-network: $2,500.
CBA Reference
Art. X, Sec. 1(a)(i) specifies that for non-Toronto Tempo players the in-network plan carries a $600 annual deductible per covered individual and the out-of-network plan carries a $2,500 annual deductible per covered individual. The $1,800 and $6,250 figures are the per-family deductibles, and $3,000/$8,000 are the out-of-pocket thresholds at which coverage reaches 100%.
Q9Art. V Sec. 3(b)T/F
True or False: Under Art. V, Sec. 3(b), a Rookie Scale Contract that contains Base Salary protection counts against the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract's initial and option term.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. V, Sec. 3(b) expressly states that any Rookie Scale Contract containing Base Salary protection shall NOT count against the per-team limit of seven (7) Player Contracts containing Base Salary protection during the four Seasons of the Rookie Scale Contract (i.e., the original three Seasons plus the option year). This exception ceases once the Rookie Scale Contract is extended.
Q10Art. VIII Sec. (b)T/F
True or False: Under Art. VIII, Sec. (b), the Rookie Scale Amount for the overall first pick in the 2026 WNBA Draft for the THIRD Season of her Rookie Scale Contract is $572,000.
ATrue
BFalse
CBA Reference
Art. VIII, Sec. (b) sets forth the 2026 Rookie Scale Amounts for the first overall pick as $500,000 (Season 1), $520,000 (Season 2), $572,000 (Season 3), and $646,360 (Season 4/Option Year). The $572,000 figure for Season 3 is therefore correct.
